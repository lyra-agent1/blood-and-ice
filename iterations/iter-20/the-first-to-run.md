# Iteration 20: The First to Run

The message burns.

The scout’s horse is already a speck on the road inland. The parchment curls in the fire I built myself, the words about a forgotten North and a throne with no Stark left to claim turning black.

My army does not sleep.

They watch the smoke the way they watched the red the first time it laughed on Skagos. The hunter from the Hornless — the one who stepped back when the lions died — stands at the edge of the firelight with two others. Their spears are in their hands but their eyes are on the trees, not on me.

The smallfolk girl from the tower sits with them. She has not spoken since the scout arrived. She looks at the place where the boy used to be and sees only the crown of bone and the thing that wears it.

I stand with the black-wood spear and Shaggydog at my heel. The direwolf’s eyes are the color of the red now. Even he does not press close.

Yrsa and Eira are the only ones who stand with me. They are the last.

The red is quiet for once. Full from the lions and the scout’s memories. It smiles in the dark and waits to see what the boy who is almost gone will do.

The hunter moves first.

He does not run like a coward. He walks to the edge of the trees with his two men and the girl, as if he still believes there is a line the red will not cross.

I feel the red lift its head.

I let it have the eyes.

They are twenty paces into the green when Shaggydog’s growl stops them. The Last Skin’s people melt out of the dark around them. Painted faces. Smiling with filed teeth.

They are brought back.

The hunter does not kneel.

He stands in front of the fire with the girl at his side and looks at me like I am already a story the island tells to frighten children.

“You are not the wolf we followed from the black rock,” he says. His voice carries. The army hears. “You are the red wearing a dead boy’s skin. The green has forgotten you. We should have left you on the sand.”

The red surges.

I do not stop it.

I step into the hunter.

His skin is old fear and older loyalty turned to ash. I see through his eyes what the army sees now — a monster in a cracked crown who laughs with too many voices and forgets the names of the men who bleed for him. I see the smallfolk girl’s face the night the tower burned. I see Yrsa’s spear between us every time the red wants more.

The red wants to keep this skin.

It wants to wear the hunter back to the army and make an example that will hold the rest.

I almost let it.

Yrsa’s voice cuts through from outside.

“Rickon.”

The name is a command.

I pull back.

The hunter falls to his knees, gasping, eyes wild with what he felt from the inside.

The girl is crying. Not loud. The way someone cries when they have already accepted the end.

Eira steps forward with the piece of the killing horn in her fist. She holds it out to me in front of everyone.

“You chose on the path,” she says. Loud enough for the army. “When the red wanted my throat. You chose the girl instead of the blood. Choose now.”

Yrsa plants her spear in the earth between me and the hunter.

“If you take him,” she says, “you take the last thing that still believes there is a Stark left to follow. The red can have the green. It cannot have us.”

The army is silent.

Even the Last Skin does not laugh.

I look at the hunter.

I look at the girl.

Inside, there is almost nothing left that remembers why any of this matters.

Only the rage.

Only the rising.

Only the throne that broke everything.

I take the piece of horn from Eira.

The red pulls back.

Not because it is tamed.

Because it knows the anchors will break before they let it have this.

I turn to the army.

“Three of you will run tonight,” I say. The voice is layers. “The girl and the two who stood with the hunter. They will carry the message the scout could not. Tell the Crossing. Tell the lions. Tell whatever is left of the North. The dead boy is gone. Only the red remains. And the red is coming for the chair anyway.”

The hunter stares at me like he has seen the end.

I point the broken spear at him.

“But you stay.”

The red takes the body for the last time that night.

I do not let it have all of him.

Enough to make it clean.

Enough to make the army remember who holds the leash.

When it is done, the body is on the ground and the red is sated.

The girl and the two men are gone into the dark with the story.

Yrsa and Eira stand where they stood.

I look at the crown of bone in my hands. Cracked. Bleeding from the skulls.

The name is gone.

The boy is gone.

What remains is the army that fears its own king.

What remains is the rising.

What remains is the throne.

At dawn we march.

The green will learn what happens when the dead decide the living have forgotten them long enough.
