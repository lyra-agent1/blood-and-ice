# Iteration 36: The Red Burns the Holdfast

The smoke reaches the Twins before the column returns.

We see it on the second day after the riders left — a thin black line against the northern sky. The painted men on the walls cheer when they see it. Some of them have painted their faces red with ash and blood. They say the chair needs no name, only the rising. They say the North will learn the same lesson the Crossing learned.

I stand on the south tower with the crown in my hand. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins. Eira stands where the red can see her. She breathes. She carries what the red left her. The army that remains watches the smoke and smiles with red in their teeth.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

The column returns at dusk on the third day.

Unicorns pull carts heavy with taken banners and the faces that would not kneel. Skinchangers walk among them wearing the faces we sent north. The old holdfast is gone. What remains is smoke and stacked dead and a new message written in the language the red has taught the green and the black and now the North.

A painted man who went with the column steps forward. His face is still half the northerner he wore.

“They came with the direwolf and the bear,” he says. “They said the lost wolf had returned to take back what the lions stole. They said the old gods would judge the red. They said Winterfell still flies the direwolf for the boy who lived.”

The red tastes the words.

Direwolf. Bear. Old gods. Winterfell. Boy who lived.

It spits them out.

I wear the painted man for a breath. Not because I need his eyes. Because the red wants to know what the holdfast tasted like when it still believed in names.

The memories are stubborn. A hall with half its roof gone. Men who still whispered the name the red ate around small fires. A girl who once ran through snow with a boy who is no longer here. The old gods watching from trees that have seen too many winters and too many dead.

The red chews until there is nothing left but the chair.

When I pull back the painted man is on his knees with blood in his mouth.

“Send more worn faces north,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “Let the next holdfast see their own men telling them the lost wolf is gone. Only the red remains. Burn what will not kneel. Stack what will not burn. We will follow the smoke.”

The painted men who smile at the red obey.

Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted the Crossing. It has tasted the North’s thin loyalty at the bridge and the Twins and the holdfast. It has eaten the last echoes of the boy who ran through snow. It wants the halls that still fly the old colors. It wants Winterfell. It wants the chair that sits in the snow and the walls that will fall when the red reaches them.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps forward.

“The lions will come from the south while we are gone,” he says. “The North may send more from the trees.”

I look at the smoke on the northern horizon.

“Let them come from both sides,” I say. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at the Twins. The chair does not care how many banners still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The Twins burn their fires higher.

The column moves north again.

Unicorns pull the carts. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the holdfast dead. The army that remains at the Twins makes the message taller so the green and the North will see it for days.

I stand on the south tower with the crown in my hand.

Eira stands where the red can see her.

Winterfell is farther.

And the red turns north with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
