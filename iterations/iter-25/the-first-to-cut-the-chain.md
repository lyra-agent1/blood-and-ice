# Iteration 25: The First to Cut the Chain

The Frey bastard screams when we start the wearing.

He is tied between two unicorns, arms stretched until the shoulders pop. The painted men have already taken his crossbow and his fingers. They want him clean for the red. His eyes roll white when the skinchanger who will carry him steps close. The man who will wear the Frey’s face has the look of someone who has already decided this is the price of the chair.

I stand apart. The crown is heavy with two pieces of the killing horn. They clack when the wind moves. The army watches the crown more than they watch me. Good. Let them see what holds the leash.

Inside the hollow, the red is still tasting the young lion.

Soft things. Lemon on a mother’s hands. A tower girl who laughed when she was afraid. A father who said the North would break a man before it made him. The red chews these and spits the pieces into the chair. It does not need soft. It needs the walls of the Crossing and the lions’ gold stacked like bodies.

It almost took Eira’s eyes at the river. It still wants them.

She stands now with Yrsa at the edge of the fireless circle. The second horn piece on the crown is hers. The first is the Bone-King’s. Both hang where every painted man can see them when they look at their king. Some look at the horns and their faces go still with something like hope. Most look and then look away.

The red laughs low in the hollow.

*They will learn. The chain is not for them. The chain is for us.*

A skinchanger crawls out of the dark. Not the one who wore the lion. Another. His face is still half green. He has been running since the river.

“The big host moves,” he says. No whisper. He is too tired for whispers. “Two thousand under the rose and the lion. They burned three villages to make the road. They have sent riders ahead to cut the fords. They know where the column is. They will be on us before the next dark if we do not turn or break them.”

The painted men shift. The ones who joined after the first raids — the ones who still smell of green fields — mutter. The old Skagosi do not. They have seen what happens when the red is leashed too long.

I take the news in the hollow way. The chair is closer. The green is finally afraid enough to move as one. Good.

“Rest the unicorns,” I say. “We march at first light. We take the old watchtower on the ridge before they can put men in it. From there we can see every road.”

The skinchanger who will wear the Frey steps forward. He is eager. The red likes that.

Before he can touch the prisoner, the knives come out.

It is not loud. Five painted men. Two of them were at the first throne. One is the brother of the Hornless hunter we executed after the desertion. The other two came from the green villages we burned. They move low and fast toward Yrsa and Eira.

They do not come for me.

They come for the chain.

“The king is not free while they live,” the hunter’s brother says. His voice is low but it carries. “The red would have ended the green at the river if not for the women and the horn. Let the red rise. Let the dead boy finish what he started. We will follow the red to the chair. We will not follow women holding a leash.”

Yrsa is already on her feet, spear down. Eira has the horn piece in her hand like a dagger. The army does not move to stop the five. Some of them watch with the same hunger the red feels.

Inside the hollow the red surges.

*Yes. Let them cut. Let the last anchors fall. Then we wear the Frey and the lion and every face between here and the walls and there will be no one left to say the name we lost.*

My hand twitches toward the crown. Not to stop them. To help them.

For one breath the boy who is almost gone feels something that is not the red. It comes from the young lion’s soft things. The tower girl. The way she laughed when she was afraid. It tastes like a name I cannot reach. Like a girl who used to run through snow with me. Like a brother who once put a crown of flowers on my head and called me little wolf.

The red eats it before the breath finishes.

There is only the chair.

Yrsa’s spear is at the hunter’s brother’s throat before he reaches Eira. The other four hesitate when they see the point draw blood.

Eira does not back away. She steps into the circle with the horn piece raised so every man can see it catch the moonlight.

“You want the red without the chain,” she says. Her voice is steady. “You think it will love you when it has the chair. It will not. It will wear you last.”

The hunter’s brother spits. “Better to be worn by a king than leashed by a woman who was never more than meat on the island.”

I move.

The red moves with me.

I take the hunter’s brother by the face the way I took the skinchanger earlier. The red slides in fast and clean. His memories are sharp — the hunter’s last look, the execution, the way the column kept marching after. He hates me. He hates the chain more.

I let the red laugh through his mouth.

Then I turn him on the two green men who came with him.

They die quick. The unicorns smell the blood and stamp. The army watches.

When it is done I let the body fall. The red wants to keep wearing. It wants to walk the camp in the hunter’s brother’s skin and find every man who still thinks the anchors are a mercy.

I pull back.

The effort leaves the hollow ringing.

Yrsa’s spear is still at my throat now. The army sees it. They see their king choose the chain again. They see the two horn pieces on the crown and the body at my feet.

Some of them kneel.

Some of them do not.

The skinchanger who was meant to wear the Frey does it anyway. He steps into the prisoner while the red is still close to the surface. The Frey’s scream becomes the skinchanger’s scream and then it becomes something else. When he opens his eyes the face is wrong in the way all worn faces are wrong. He looks at me with the Frey’s mean eyes and the red’s hunger.

“Send him ahead,” I say. “Let the big host hear their own man telling them the dead boy king is already inside their lines. Let them wonder which of their brothers is still theirs.”

The worn Frey nods and slips into the dark.

At first light the column moves.

The watchtower on the ridge is a ruin. Stark stone, half fallen, the wolf banner long since rotted to threads. From the top you can see three roads and the smoke of the green host’s fires. They are closer than the skinchanger said. Less than a day now. Their outriders will reach us by midday.

The army makes no fires. They stack the two green men the way we stack all the dead. High enough to be seen.

Yrsa and Eira stand on either side of me again. The crown clicks when I turn my head. The chain of horn is visible to every man who looks up.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is thinking.

It tasted the tower girl again when it wore the hunter’s brother. It tasted the almost-name. It let me pull back because the anchors are still useful. Because the army is not yet ready to follow the red without anything human left to point it at the chair.

But it is patient.

It is patient the way a fire is patient when the forest is already dry and the wind is rising.

The worn Frey will bring them to us.

The big host will come.

And when they do, the chain will either hold or it will not.

The chair does not care which.

Only the rising does.
