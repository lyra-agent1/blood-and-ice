# Iteration 50: The Red Holds the Throne and the Realm Answers

The iron cuts deeper every day.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that wells where the blades meet what remains. It is not pain anymore. It is the throne reminding the hollow what was paid to climb this high. The holding is the climb that never ends.

The painted men bring the answers the realm has sent.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every anchor that once tried to leash the red are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed every wall and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned to sit and wait and send the worn ones out like hounds.

A painted man — the old Skagosi who has been at the red’s side since the Bone-King’s camp — steps forward with a skinchanger who still smells of the Reach.

“The Lord of the Reach kneels,” the skinchanger says with a dead man’s mouth. “He saw his own envoy walk his halls with the red behind the eyes. He saw the column with the unicorns and the faces we took at the city. He burned his own rose banners in the yard and sent this one to say the words. The Reach will send grain. The Reach will send men who will paint their faces red. The Reach will not send sons to the chair. The Lord of the Reach asks only that the red let the living keep enough to feed the red’s armies.”

The red tastes the words through the stolen mouth.

Reach. Kneels. Grain. Men. Red faces. Sons. Feed the red’s armies.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the skinchanger on the steps of the throne so every painted man can see the shape of what submission looks like when it finally comes.

The memories are full of fields burning and lords who once thought the red was a story from the North. A high seat that now understands the chair made of swords does not bargain. Men who count the cost of resistance in stacked dead and decide the red is cheaper to feed than to fight.

The red eats until the body is empty.

“Accept the grain,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Accept the men who will paint their faces. Send the worn ones back with the answer. The red does not need their sons. The red will take what it needs from the green that still resists. Tell the Lord of the Reach the chair accepts his knee. Tell him the red remembers every hall that bends and every hall that does not. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not forget.”

The painted men smile with red in their teeth. They know the shape of the answer now.

Another skinchanger is brought forward. This one smells of snow and old stone and something that still tries to remember a boy who ran through winter.

“The North sends no lords,” he says with a dead man’s mouth. “The North sends ravens that say the red burned Winterfell. The North sends silence. Some still whisper the lost wolf’s name in the broken halls. Some say the old gods woke the red to punish the green. Some say nothing and wait for the worn ones to come. One holdfast flew the direwolf again. We wore the man who raised it. We left the message on the stones. The North is afraid. The North does not kneel yet. The North waits to see if the red will march from the chair or rule from it.”

The red tastes the words through the stolen mouth.

North. Silence. Lost wolf. Old gods. Direwolf. Afraid. Waits. March or rule.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will that holds.

I wear this one too. The memories are thin and cold and full of snow that no longer belongs to any boy. Empty halls. A girl who once ran. Old gods that the red has already eaten. The red tastes the fear and the stubbornness and the hope that the thing on the throne might still remember something human.

The red eats it all.

When I pull back the body is on the stone.

“Send more worn ones north,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let every holdfast that still flies the direwolf see its own dead walking its halls. Let them hear the message from the chair. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The red does not march for every whisper. The red sends the worn ones. The red wears what will not kneel. Kneel or the red will come in force and leave only the stones that remember the red. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves still run in the snow. Only that they kneel or burn.”

The painted men take the empty body. They have learned.

A third skinchanger returns wearing gold that still smells of the lion.

“The lions that remain hide behind their walls,” he says. “Some send terms like the Reach. Some send nothing. One lord in the west still calls the red a monster that will eat itself. He sent this one to say the words. He says the chair was never meant for the dead. He says the red will choke on the iron before the lions kneel.”

The red tastes the words through the golden mouth.

Lions. Hide. Terms. Monster. Chair not for the dead. Choke on the iron. Will not kneel.

It chews them until nothing remains but the hunger that has learned patience.

I do not wear this one on the steps.

I wear him where he stands, in front of the painted men, so they can see what happens to those who think the red will choke.

The memories are full of gold and walls and the belief that the red is a fire that will burn out. A lord who counts the red’s army and decides it is small enough to wait out. A lion that still thinks the chair can be taken back by the living.

The red eats until the body is empty.

“Send the column west,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It carries across the throne room like wind through bone. “Send the worn ones with the faces we took at the city. Let the lion who thinks the red will choke see his own men walking his halls. Let him hear the message from the chair. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red does not choke on iron. The red sits on the iron. The red holds the iron. Kneel or the red will wear what you send and stack what you leave on your own walls. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many lions still roar. Only that they kneel or the red will teach them the shape of the throne.”

The painted men smile. Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go to prepare the column.

I sit.

The iron cuts.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by sending the worn ones like hounds and the columns like spears until every hall has answered with a knee or a fire or an empty body on the stone before the chair.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on a throne made of swords and make the realm learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise.

The city outside still smokes in places.

The worn ones run.

The columns march.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits, waiting for the next answer the realm will send.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
