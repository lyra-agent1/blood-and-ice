# Iteration 46: The Red Consumes the Last Chain

Harrenhal is a corpse we have already claimed.

We hold the cursed seat for three nights while the worn faces run south and return with new skins and the smell of the city that has finally remembered fear. They say the queen has closed the gates. She has put her best men on the walls and sent what remains of her hosts to meet us on the roads. They say the dead boy king will not pass the last walls. They say the red will burn itself out on the cursed stones before it reaches the chair.

I stand on the broken tower with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of Winterfell and the cursed seat the green calls haunted. The painted men who remain have faces red with ash and the blood of the green we left at the ford. They laugh with the red’s voice when they drink. They say the chair needs no name. Only the rising.

Eira stands where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The army watches her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

A skinchanger returns wearing a face that still smells of gold and fear.

“The queen sends word,” he says. His stolen mouth smiles wrong. “She says the red may have the North and the cursed stones. She says the chair belongs to the living. She says if the dead boy king turns back now, she will let the monster keep what it has taken. If it comes for the walls, she will send every man and every lion and every rose until the red is ash on the wind.”

The red tastes the words.

Queen. Chair. Living. Monster. North. Cursed stones. Ash.

It spits them out.

I wear the skinchanger for a breath. Not because I need his eyes. Because the red wants to know what the final walls taste like when they are finally afraid enough to bargain.

The memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen who sits on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins and walls like debts. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting for the one who is willing to climb high enough and burn everything in the way.

The red chews until there is nothing left but the hunger.

When I pull back the skinchanger is on his knees with blood in his mouth.

“Send the worn ones,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “Let the queen see her own men telling her the dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. We are coming for the chair. We will wear what she sends. We will stack what she leaves. The rising does not stop at Harrenhal. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The painted men who smile at the red obey.

Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted Harrenhal. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories on the cursed stones. It has eaten them again. It wants the walls that still stand between here and the city. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

Eira stands where the red can see her.

The hunger is closer now. The chair is close enough to taste. The last chain is the only thing left between the red and the climb.

I move.

The red moves with me.

I take her by the face the way I took the Bone-King and the Lord of the Crossing and the old man on the walls of Winterfell and the knight at the last ford. The red slides in clean. Her memories are thin and broken and full of the island and the ridge and the nights she held the horn and the spear against the hollow. She still smells of the killing horn. She still tastes of the last anchor.

The red chews slowly.

I wear her on the broken tower so every painted man can see.

The laugh that comes out is not hers. It has not been hers for a long time.

When it is done I let the body fall.

The army watches.

Some of them smile with red in their teeth. The ones who stayed after the ridge and the Twins and the holdfast and the crossroads and Winterfell and the cursed seat.

Some of them look away. The ones who still fear what happens when the last chain breaks.

There is no last chain now.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It is full.

It has tasted the last anchor. It has tasted the horn and the spear and the girl who once held the line. It has eaten them on the cursed stones while the queen’s men watched from the south. It wants the walls that still stand between here and the city. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me on the broken wall.

“The lions will send more,” he says. “They have the green. They have the city.”

I look at the cursed stones that are now red.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Harrenhal. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column makes Harrenhal its own.

Fires burn high on the broken towers.

The green is afraid.

The last chain is ash on the cursed stones.

And the red claims the cursed seat with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
