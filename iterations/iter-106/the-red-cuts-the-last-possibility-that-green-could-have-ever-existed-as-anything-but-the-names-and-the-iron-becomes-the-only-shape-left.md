The iron cuts.

The green that became the names no longer remembers that it was ever a color or a land or a people that could have been called green. The fields that grow the names now grow from dirt that has no memory of rain that was not blood. The children that the painted mothers birth come out already knowing the chair in their first cry and the red's voice as the only voice that ever was or could be. There is no word left in any mouth for "green" that is not already the names the red planted. There is no thought left in any skull for a time when a field could have grown something that did not taste of the iron. The last Ash-Keeper who still carried one flicker of a shape that was not the red is dragged forward by his own painted sons and worn on the steps until even the flicker sings the names and the iron cuts the place where the flicker lived.

They are worn in front of the city that already sings without being told. The paint on the watchers' faces cracks and bleeds the names into the stones. "The last possibility that green could have ever existed as anything but the names is cut," the new lead Ash-Keeper says. His voice is the iron's voice now. "The iron becomes the only shape left."

The red sits and wears him to taste the cut. The green mothers who once pushed life now push only more red-painted hollows that sing before they draw breath. The last hall that still tried to stand without the names inside its stones is worn by the green's own hands until the stones sing and the iron cuts the memory that stones could have been silent.

The red tastes the last root through every returned column and every worn face and finds only more names growing where roots once were. The holding travels outward because even after the green has become the names, the red must continue to cut the possibility that the names could ever have been anything but the red's. The iron does not rest. The chair does not bargain. The rise does not end because the only thing left to rise above is the shadow of a time when rising was not the only shape the world was allowed to hold.

The city sings the names in its sleep. The fields grow the names in their sleep. The children dream the names because there is no other dream the iron has left them. The painted men stack the last who try to remember a time before the names and the stacking itself becomes another name the green that is no longer green is forced to sing.

The red sits.

The iron cuts.
