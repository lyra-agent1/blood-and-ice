# Iteration 41: The Red Meets the Green

The Kingsroad runs red before the sun reaches its height.

The worn faces return at dawn with new skins and the smell of gold. They say the green host is larger than the one that came for us at the ridge. Gold and rose and the black of the Crossing that is no longer black. They have sent men to cut the roads behind us and men to hold the fords ahead. They mean to trap the red between the North that is now ash and the walls that still stand in the south.

I ride at the head of the column with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of the boy’s own home. The unicorns pull the carts with the taken banners and the faces that would not kneel. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the northern dead.

Eira walks where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The painted men who remain watch her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

We meet them on a stretch of road where the trees are thin and the old gods cannot watch.

They come with shields locked and banners high. A knight in gold and red at the front. A man in black who still calls himself Frey. They stop when they see the unicorns and the worn faces walking among us like the dead returned.

“Rickon Stark!” the knight calls. His voice carries across the road. “The realm sent us to bring the dead boy to justice. You burned your own home. You took the Crossing. You have become the monster the stories warned us of. Lay down your arms and the lions will be merciful.”

The name does not touch the hollow.

The red has eaten it.

I send the first answer without words.

Skinchangers wearing the faces from Winterfell and the crossroads slip into their lines at the edge of bowshot. They walk among the green men wearing northern skins and black. By the time the knight notices the wrongness in the eyes of his own scouts, the red is already inside their shield wall.

The laugh starts low in the hollow when the first horn sounds.

I ride into the green with the unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red. The knight tries to rally his men. I take him by the throat the way I took the Bone-King and the Lord of the Crossing and the old man on the walls of Winterfell.

His memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen who sits on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting.

The red chews them slowly.

I wear the knight in front of his own host.

“The dead boy is gone,” I say with his voice. The red makes it carry across the road. “Only the red remains. The chair does not care which banners still fly. Only that we climb.”

The green host breaks.

Some of them run. The unicorns take them on the road. Some of them kneel. The painted men who smile at the red stack the ones who do not kneel the Skagosi way.

When it is done the gold and rose and black banners are ash on the wind.

I stand on the road with the crown in my hand. The stacked dead are higher than they were at the Twins. The painted men work quickly. They have learned the shape of the message.

Eira is brought forward. She does not resist. The red looks at her through my eyes and considers finishing what it started on the ridge. It decides against it. She is still useful. The army still watches her. A living reminder of what the chain looked like before it broke and the red was given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It has tasted the green host. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories. It has eaten them again. It wants the walls that still stand in the south. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me on the road.

“The lions will send more,” he says. “They have the green. They have the numbers.”

I look at the banners burning on the Kingsroad.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column moves south again.

The worn faces run ahead wearing the faces we took from the green dead. The unicorns pull the carts. The painted men who remain march with red on their faces and the laugh in their teeth.

Eira walks where the red can see her.

The Twins are a skull on the river ahead.

The green is afraid.

And the red marches with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
