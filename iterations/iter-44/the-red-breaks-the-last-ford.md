# Iteration 44: The Red Breaks the Last Ford

The river is wide and brown and full of the green’s dead before we reach the far bank.

We leave the Twins on the third day after the message went south. The worn faces run ahead wearing gold and rose and black. They return with new skins and the smell of the city that has forgotten the North. They say the lions have put their best at the last ford. Gold and rose and the black that is no longer black. They have archers on the high ground and spears in the water and a knight who still calls himself Lannister at the front. They say the dead boy king will not cross. They say the red will drown in its own hunger before it reaches the chair.

I ride at the head of the column with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of Winterfell. The unicorns pull the carts with the taken banners and the faces that would not kneel. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the green dead on the road.

Eira walks where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The painted men who remain have faces red with ash and the blood of the knights we broke on the Kingsroad. They watch her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

The green host waits on the far bank with shields locked and banners high. The knight in gold and red calls across the water.

“Rickon Stark! The realm sent us to end this madness. You burned your own home. You took the Crossing. You have become the monster the stories warned us of. Turn back or we will send what remains of you to the queen in a box. The dead have no claim on the living.”

The name does not touch the hollow.

The red has eaten it.

I send the first answer without words.

Skinchangers wearing the faces from the Twins and the road slip into the water at dusk. They climb the far bank wearing green men’s skins and gold. By the time the archers on the high ground notice the wrongness in the eyes of their own scouts, the red is already inside their lines.

The laugh starts low in the hollow when the first horn sounds.

I ride into the ford with the unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red. The river runs the color the red likes best before the first spear finds me. I take the knight by the throat the way I took the Bone-King and the Lord of the Crossing and the old man on the walls of Winterfell.

His memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen who sits on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting for the one who is willing to climb high enough.

The red chews them slowly.

I wear the knight in front of his own host.

“The dead boy is gone,” I say with his voice. The red makes it carry across the water. “Only the red remains. The chair does not care which banners still fly. Only that we climb.”

The green host breaks.

Some of them run. The unicorns take them in the water. Some of them kneel. The painted men who smile at the red stack the ones who do not kneel the Skagosi way on the far bank.

When it is done the gold and rose and black banners are ash on the wind.

I stand on the captured bank with the crown in my hand. The stacked dead are higher than they were at the Twins. The painted men work quickly. They have learned the shape of the message.

Eira is brought forward. She does not resist. The red looks at her through my eyes and considers finishing what it started on the ridge. It has considered this every night since the Twins. It decides against it again. She is still useful. The army still watches her. A living reminder of what the chain looked like before it broke and the red was given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It has tasted the green host at the last ford. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories. It has eaten them again. It wants the walls that still stand between here and the city. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me on the bank.

“The lions will send more,” he says. “They have the green. They have the city.”

I look at the banners burning on the water.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column crosses before the light dies.

The worn faces run ahead wearing the faces we took from the green dead at the ford. The unicorns pull the carts. The painted men who remain march with red on their faces and the laugh in their teeth.

Eira walks where the red can see her.

The green is afraid.

The chair waits in the south.

And the red marches with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
