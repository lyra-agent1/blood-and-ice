# Iteration 57: The Red Holds While the Snow Burns

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that has become the throne’s own. Every day the blades dig deeper. Every day the hollow learns that the climb did not end when the last wall fell or the last godswood burned. The holding is the climb that never stops. The red sits and the realm bleeds so the chair can remain.

The painted men bring the answer from the snow.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every voice that once tried to pull the red back are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed from Skagos to the city and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned to sit and send fire until even the land that remembered the boy has no choice but to kneel or be eaten.

A column returns from the North. Fewer again. The unicorns pull carts loaded with ash and the faces that turned and the faces that did not turn. Some of the painted men who left with red on their faces come back with the snow still in their eyes. Some do not come back at all. The ones who return bring burned branches from the last godswoods and the heads of the last skinchangers who still tried to fight the red and the message that the snow has finally learned the price of biting.

The old Skagosi steps forward. His face is painted fresh red over scars the fire and the snow gave him.

“The snow burns,” he says. “We sent every face the red had taken. We sent the last fire. We wore the last skinchangers who tried to stop us. We burned the last godswoods the North still whispered to. The old gods moved through the flames one last time. The land itself tried to pull the red back into the snow. Skinchangers loyal to the North wore the faces we sent and turned them on the column until the end. We lost more painted men. We lost more faces the red had already eaten. But we broke the last holdfasts. We stacked the ones who would not kneel on the stones where the wolf once flew. The snow is ash. The old gods are ash. The land still bites where it can but the red sent more fire than the snow could eat. The last voice that remembered the boy is ash on the wind.”

The red tastes the words.

Snow. Burns. Old gods. Last flames. Land itself tried to pull the red back. Lost more painted men. Lost more faces. Broke the last holdfasts. Stacked the ones who would not kneel. Snow is ash. Old gods are ash. Land still bites. Red sent more fire than the snow could eat. Last voice that remembered the boy is ash on the wind.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the returning skinchanger on the steps of the Iron Throne so every painted man in the hall can see what the snow’s last resistance tastes like when the red finally reaches it.

The memories are cold and full of the snow the boy once loved. Pine and wind and the last godswoods that tried to whisper names the red has already eaten. Skinchangers who still believed the lost wolf might return as something that remembers the cold. The old gods moving through the burning trees one final time, trying to pull the red back into a body that could feel the snow and the names and the girl who ran. The land itself fighting the faces the red sent and sending them back with knives and the old gods’ hate until the last tree fell. The hope that the thing on the chair might still have a heart the snow could freeze.

The red eats slowly.

It eats the pine and the wind. It eats the last godswoods that tried to whisper. It eats the skinchangers who thought the snow could wear the red’s tools and live. It eats the old gods that tried to move through the fire and pull the boy back from the chair one last time.

When it is done the body is on the stone before the throne.

The painted men take it. They have learned the shape of the snow’s answers.

“Leave the worn ones in the snow,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Leave the painted men who still smile at the red without the snow in their eyes. Let the snow see the faces we left behind every time it thinks of biting the red again. Let the old gods see the faces we left behind every time the wind moves through the ash where their trees once stood. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The snow is ash. The old gods are ash. The land will learn the shape of the red that climbed. The worn ones will wear what the snow tries to raise. The columns will return when the snow has nothing left to send. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves once ran in the snow or how many trees once whispered the old names. Only that they kneel or the red will eat what remains of the snow and leave only the stones that remember the red.”

The old Skagosi paints more red on his face before he turns. The painted men who smile at the red without the snow in their eyes follow him to prepare the ones who will stay behind. They know the shape of the order now.

Another painted man steps forward. This one smells of the west and the gold the lions continue to send.

“The lions send more gold,” he says. “The Reach sends more grain and more men with red on their faces. The city still smokes where the smallfolk tried to hide what they could not give. The worn ones walk every hall that bent. The realm learns the shape of the chair. The snow is ash. The old gods are ash. The red holds the North from the chair.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Gold. Reach. Grain. Red faces. City. Smokes. Smallfolk. Worn ones. Realm learns. Snow is ash. Old gods are ash. Red holds the North from the chair.

It chews them until nothing remains but the holding.

“Good,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let the lions feed the red with gold. Let the Reach feed the red with grain and men who paint their faces. Let the city smoke where it must. The worn ones will find what the smallfolk try to hide. The painted men will stack what the worn ones leave. The chair does not care what the snow once was. Only that the snow learns the same lesson the green learned. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not rest while any land still thinks it can bite the red and live.”

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper into what remains.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by leaving faces in the ash and sending fire until every land that ever tried to remember the boy has no choice but to kneel or burn or be eaten by the red that sits on the throne made of a thousand swords.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name and a sister who ran through snow and old gods that spoke in the trees.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on the chair and make the realm — even the snow, even the old gods — learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise against the climb that never ends.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes in the places the smallfolk learned the cost of hiding.

The worn ones run north with the faces the red left behind to watch the ash.

The columns begin the long march back from the snow with fire in their wake.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne, waiting for the next answer the realm will send.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
