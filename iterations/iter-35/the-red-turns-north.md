# Iteration 35: The Red Turns North

The Twins are quiet in the way a skull is quiet after the meat is gone.

We have held them for seven days. The banners are ash. The dead are stacked on both bridges and along the river banks high enough that the smoke writes the message for anyone with eyes. The painted men who remained after the ridge and the ford have taken the black halls as their own. They wear the Frey faces when they walk the walls. They laugh with the red’s voice when they drink.

Eira stands in the Lord’s hall where I can see her. She does not speak. She has not spoken since the ridge. The red left her legs and her eyes and enough of her mind to understand that she is the last chain, and the chain is broken. The army watches her when they think I do not see. Some of them smile. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

A skinchanger returns at dusk wearing a face that still smells of the Neck.

“More riders,” he says. His stolen mouth is wrong. “They gather at the old holdfast two days north. They fly the direwolf and the bear and the flayed man’s ghost. They say the lost wolf has come to take the North back from the lions and the dead. They say Winterfell still stands for the boy who lived.”

The red tastes the words.

Holdfast. Direwolf. Bear. Flayed man. Winterfell. Lost wolf. Boy who lived.

It spits them out.

I wear the skinchanger for a breath. Not because I need his eyes. Because the red wants to know what the North tastes like when it still believes the dead can wear crowns.

The memories are stubborn and thin. A hall with half its roof gone. Men who still whisper the name the red ate around small fires. A girl who once ran through snow with a boy who is no longer here. The old gods watching from trees that have seen too many winters.

The red chews until there is nothing left but the chair.

When I pull back the skinchanger is on his knees with blood in his mouth.

“Send the column,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “Take the unicorns and the worn faces. Let the holdfast see their own men telling them the lost wolf is gone. Only the red remains. Burn what will not kneel. Stack what will not burn. We will follow when the smoke reaches the Twins.”

The painted men who smile at the red obey.

Some of them have begun to paint their own faces red. They say the chair needs no name. Only the rising.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted the Crossing. It has tasted the North’s thin loyalty at the bridge and the Twins. It has eaten the last echoes of the boy who ran through snow. It wants the holdfast. It wants the halls that still fly the old colors. It wants Winterfell. It wants the chair that sits in the snow and the walls that will fall when the red reaches them.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps forward.

“The lions will come from the south while we are gone,” he says. “The North may send more from the trees.”

I look at the black walls that are now red.

“Let them come from both sides,” I say. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at the Twins. The chair does not care how many banners still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column leaves at first light.

Unicorns pull the carts with the worn faces and the taken banners. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the Frey sons and the northern riders. The army that remains at the Twins makes the fires higher so the smoke will be seen for days.

I stand on the south tower with the crown in my hand. No horns. No chains. Only the light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the direwolf riders who came to see if the dead could still wear the old colors.

Eira stands where the red can see her.

The North is two days away.

Winterfell is farther.

And the red turns north with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
