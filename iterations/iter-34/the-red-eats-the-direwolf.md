# Iteration 34: The Red Eats the Direwolf

The northern riders come on the fifth day after the Twins fell.

They fly the direwolf on faded cloth. Twenty men, maybe thirty, with the look of survivors who have already lost more than they can count. Their leader is old, with a beard gone white at the edges and a sword that has seen too many winters. He stops his horse at the edge of the stacked dead on the bridge and calls across the water.

“Rickon Stark! Son of Eddard! The North sent us to see if the lost wolf has truly returned. We heard the green host broke at the ridge. We heard the Crossing is yours now. Show us the boy who lived.”

The name carries on the wind.

Inside the hollow the red tastes it and finds only ash.

I stand on the south tower wall so every rider can see the crown. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins. The painted men who remain stand with me. Some wear the faces we took from the Frey sons. Some simply smile with red in their teeth.

The old northerner’s face changes when he sees the stacked dead. When he sees the fires. When he sees what is left of Eira where she stands behind the crown.

“Boy,” he says. His voice cracks on the word. “What have they done to you?”

I send the first answer without words.

Skinchangers wearing the faces from the bridge and the Twins slip across the river at dusk. They walk among the northern riders wearing green men’s skins and black. By the time the old man notices the wrongness in the eyes of his own scouts, the red is already inside their lines.

The laugh starts low in the hollow when the first horn sounds from the north bank.

I cross with the unicorns and the painted men who no longer flinch. The direwolf banner falls before the old man can draw his sword. I take him by the throat the way I took the Bone-King on the island.

His memories are thin and stubborn. Snow that never melts. A hall that still flies the direwolf in secret. Men who whisper the name the red ate and wonder if the dead can still wear the old colors. A girl who once ran through the snow with a boy who is no longer here.

The red chews them slowly.

I wear the old man in front of his own riders.

“ The lost wolf is gone,” I say with his voice. The red makes it carry across the water. “Only the red remains. The North will remember what it chooses to remember. Or it will be stacked like the rest.”

Some of the riders break.

Some of them kneel.

The ones who kneel I let live long enough to carry the message back.

The ones who do not kneel die on the bank with the unicorns.

When it is done the direwolf banner is ash on the wind.

I stand on the captured bank with the crown in my hand. The Twins watch from both sides. The stacked dead are higher now. The painted men work quickly. They have learned the shape of the message.

Eira is brought forward. She does not speak. She stands where the red can see her and the army can see what happens when the last chain is cut and the red is given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It has tasted the North’s thin loyalty. It has tasted the snow and the girl and the hall that still flies the old colors. It has eaten them again. It wants more. It wants the men who still whisper the name in secret halls. It wants Winterfell. It wants the chair that sits in the snow.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi — steps beside me.

“The lions will come from the south,” he says. “More of the North may come from the trees. The chair is still far.”

I look at the direwolf banner burning on the water.

“Let them come,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at the Twins. The chair does not care how many banners still fly the old colors. Only that we climb.”

The column makes the Twins its own.

Fires burn high in both towers.

The green is afraid.

The North has seen what the lost wolf has become.

And the red looks north with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
