# Iteration 54: The Red Sends Fire to the Snow

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that leaks where the blades meet what remains. Every day the throne reminds the hollow that the climb did not end when the last wall fell. The holding is the climb that never stops. The red sits and the realm bleeds so the chair can remain.

The painted men bring the answer from the snow.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every voice that once tried to call the red back are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed from the island to the city and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned to sit and send fire and faces to do the work of the rising.

A column returns from the North. Fewer than left. The unicorns pull carts loaded with the dead we took and the dead that took us. The painted men who smile at the red come back with faces that are not all their own. Some of them walk wrong. Some of them smile with the red’s voice but their eyes remember the snow.

The old Skagosi steps forward. His face is painted fresh red over old blood.

“The snow fights harder than the green ever did,” he says. “We wore their captains. We wore their skinchangers. We burned the holdfasts that would not kneel. But the land pushed back. The old gods moved through the trees we set on fire. Skinchangers loyal to the North wore the faces we sent and turned them on the column. Men who painted their faces red woke with knives in the dark and the North’s voice in their mouths. The snow itself rose. The trees remembered the boy who ran. The old gods do not want the red on the chair. We lost good painted men. We lost faces the red had already eaten. The North bites and does not let go.”

The red tastes the words.

Snow. Fights harder. Old gods. Trees on fire. Skinchangers loyal to the North. Wore the faces we sent. Turned them on the column. Knives in the dark. North’s voice. Snow itself rose. Trees remembered the boy. Old gods do not want the red. Lost good painted men. Lost faces. North bites and does not let go.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the returning skinchanger on the steps of the Iron Throne so every painted man in the hall can see what the snow’s defiance tastes like when it finally reaches the chair.

The memories are cold and full of the snow the boy once loved. Pine and wind and godswood that still whispers names the red has already eaten. Skinchangers who still believe the lost wolf might return as something that remembers the cold. The old gods moving through the burning trees like they moved on Skagos, but now against the red. The land itself rejecting the faces the red sent and sending them back with knives. The hope that the thing on the chair might still have a body the snow could claim.

The red eats slowly.

It eats the pine and the wind. It eats the godswood that still whispers. It eats the skinchangers who thought the North could wear the red’s tools and live. It eats the land that still remembers a boy who ran through snow and tries to pull that boy back from the chair.

When it is done the body is on the stone before the throne.

The painted men take it. They have learned the shape of the snow’s answers.

“Send fire,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Send every unicorn that remains. Send every painted man who still smiles at the red without the snow in his eyes. Send every face we took from the green and the Reach and the lions and the last who resisted the chair. Let the snow see what the red sends when the snow bites back. Let the old gods see what the red does when the land remembers a boy who no longer exists. Burn the godswoods. Wear every skinchanger the North has left. Stack what will not kneel on the stones where the wolf once flew. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The snow will kneel. The old gods will kneel. The land will learn the shape of the red that climbed. If the snow will not come to the red, the red will send fire until the snow has no choice but to come or burn. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves still run in the snow or how many trees still whisper the old names. Only that they kneel or the red will eat the snow itself and leave only the stones that remember the red.”

The old Skagosi paints more red on his face before he turns. The painted men who smile at the red without question follow him. They know the shape of the order now.

Another painted man steps forward. This one smells of the west and the gold the lions continue to send.

“The lions send more gold,” he says. “The Reach sends more grain and more men with red on their faces. The city still smokes where the smallfolk tried to hide what they could not give. The worn ones walk every hall that bent. The realm learns the shape of the chair. But the snow bites harder than any green lord ever did.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Gold. Reach. Grain. Red faces. City. Smokes. Smallfolk. Worn ones. Realm learns. Snow bites harder.

It chews them until nothing remains but the holding.

“Good,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let the lions feed the red with gold. Let the Reach feed the red with grain and men who paint their faces. Let the city smoke where it must. The worn ones will find what the smallfolk try to hide. The painted men will stack what the worn ones leave. The chair does not care what the snow does. Only that the snow learns the same lesson the green learned. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not rest while any land still thinks it can bite the red and live.”

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper into what remains.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by sending fire and faces and columns until even the snow that remembers the boy and the old gods that still whisper have no choice but to kneel or burn or be eaten by the red that sits on the throne made of a thousand swords.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name and a sister who ran through snow and old gods that spoke in the trees.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on the chair and make the realm — even the snow, even the old gods — learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise against the climb that never ends.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes in the places the smallfolk learned the cost of hiding.

The worn ones run north with every face the red has taken and the order to burn the godswoods.

The columns march north with fire and unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne, waiting for the snow to send its next answer or burn.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
