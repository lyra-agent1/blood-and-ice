# Iteration 12: The Killing Horn

The crown of bone does not sleep.

It pulses against my skull even when I try to tear it off. The skulls whisper in a tongue older than the Starks. They remember the last wolf who wore the red. They remember how he ended.

I do not sleep the night after I take it.

At dawn the storm returns.

Not the rain. The Stormfather himself.

He comes with the last of the free tribes — the ones who still call the island by its true name. They bring the killing horn, a thing of white bone and black iron that sings when it is raised. The sound cuts the red inside me like a knife through old scar tissue.

The Last Skin falls to her knees when she hears it. Blood runs from her ears.

“He means to unmake you,” she says. “The horn was made for the first red wolf. It will make the island forget you ever walked here.”

I stand on the ridge with the crown of bone on my head and Shaggydog at my side. The direwolf’s eyes are no longer yellow. They are the color of the red when it laughs.

Below us the camp is chaos. Men who knelt yesterday are running. The Bone-King is shouting orders that no one hears. Yrsa stands at the front with her spear, Eira beside her, both of them looking up at me like they are already mourning the boy I was.

I raise the black-wood spear.

The red answers.

I do not crack the door this time.

I kick it open.

The world goes scarlet and bright. I am laughing before the first of the Stormfather’s men reach the slope. The laughter is not mine. It belongs to every wolf the island ever woke and then tried to kill.

I step into the first man.

His skin fits poorly. He is older, afraid. I make him turn his own whale-tooth knife on the man beside him. The red likes that. It likes wearing enemies until they break their own.

The Stormfather sees me coming.

He lifts the killing horn.

The sound is wrong. It is the sound of Winterfell burning. It is the sound of Robb’s head on a spike. It is the sound of the boy I was screaming as the world took everything.

For one heartbeat the red falters.

I feel the crown of bone crack against my skull. Blood pours into my eyes. I see my own hands — small, child’s hands — reaching for a mother whose face I can no longer remember.

The Stormfather smiles.

He thinks he has won.

He does not understand what Darrow understood.

You do not kill the red by making it remember what it lost.

You kill it by giving it something new to hate.

I reach for the Last Skin.

Not to wear her. To pull her with me.

She screams as I drag her into the red. Together we are inside the Stormfather before he can blow the horn again.

I see what he is. An old skinchanger who gave everything to keep the island free of Starks. He has no wife. No children. Only the horn and the hate.

I take the hate from him.

I make it mine.

When we fall back into our bodies the Stormfather is on his knees, the killing horn shattered in his hands. The red is wearing his face and laughing through my mouth.

His own men turn on him.

The battle ends in minutes.

When it is over I stand among the dead with the crown of bone still on my head. Blood runs from my ears and nose and the cracks in the skulls. Yrsa is alive. Eira is alive. Shaggydog is limping but with me.

The Bone-King approaches slowly.

He does not offer another crown.

He simply goes to one knee in the mud and blood.

“King of the Red,” he says through Yrsa. “King of Skagos.”

The Last Skin laughs until she cries.

I look out over the black water.

The boy who washed up here is almost gone.

What remains is the thing the island made.

What remains is rising.

And the Iron Throne is waiting on the other side.
