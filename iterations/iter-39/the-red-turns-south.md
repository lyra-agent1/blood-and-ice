# Iteration 39: The Red Turns South

Winterfell burns red for three nights.

Not the stone. The stone has seen too many fires and too many dead. The banners burn. The last direwolf cloth is ash on the wind before the first dawn. The painted men stack the northern dead on the broken walls high enough that the smoke writes the message for anyone who still believes in old colors. Some of them have painted their faces red with ash and the blood of the last men who called the name on the stones of their own home.

I stand in the great hall with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls that once flew the direwolf for a boy who is no longer here.

Eira stands where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The army that remains watches her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

A skinchanger returns wearing a face that still smells of the south.

“The lions move,” he says. His stolen mouth smiles wrong. “They have heard what happened at the ridge and the Twins and the holdfast and the crossroads and now here. They say the dead boy king has burned his own home. They say the red is coming for the chair. They are calling their banners. They are closing the roads. They are sending men north to meet us before we can reach the green.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Chair. Dead boy king. Red. Banners. Roads.

It spits them out.

I wear the skinchanger for a breath. Not because I need his eyes. Because the red wants to know what the south tastes like when it is finally afraid enough to move as one.

The memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins. The smell of the city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting.

The red chews until there is nothing left but the hunger.

When I pull back the skinchanger is on his knees with blood in his mouth.

“Burn the rest,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “Stack what will not burn. Let the smoke be seen from the Twins and beyond. We march south at first light. The North is ash. The chair waits in the south.”

The painted men who smile at the red obey.

Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go to the walls.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted Winterfell. It has tasted the snow and the girl and the brother with the flowers on the very stones the boy came from. It has eaten them again. It wants the gold and the queen and the men who count bodies like coins. It wants the chair that sits above all of it. It wants the walls that will fall when the red reaches them.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me in the hall.

“The lions will be waiting,” he says. “They have numbers. They have the green.”

I look at the broken towers and the snow that has already begun to cover the stacked dead.

“Let them wait,” I say. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many banners still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column leaves Winterfell at first light.

Unicorns pull the carts with the worn faces and the taken banners. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the northern dead. The army that remains is smaller than the one that left the beachhead, but it is harder. Every man who stayed after the ridge and the ford and the Twins and the holdfast and the crossroads and the walls of the boy’s own home knows what happens when the red is given nothing to fear.

Eira walks with the column.

The Kingsroad stretches south like a wound the North never learned to close.

Winterfell is smoke and ash behind us.

The Twins are a skull on the river ahead.

And the red turns south with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
