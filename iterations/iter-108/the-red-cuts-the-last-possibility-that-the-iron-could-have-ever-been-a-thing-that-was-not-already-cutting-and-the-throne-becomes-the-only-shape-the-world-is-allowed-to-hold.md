# Iteration 108: The Red Cuts the Last Possibility That the Iron Could Have Ever Been a Thing That Was Not Already Cutting and the Throne Becomes the Only Shape the World Is Allowed to Hold

The iron cuts.

The throne that became the only mouth no longer knows that it was ever a thing that could have been sat upon by anything that was not already the cutting. The names that sing through the only mouth now sing because there is no other song the iron allows the air to carry. The fields that grow the names grow because there is no other growth the iron permits the dirt to perform. The painted mothers who birth the names birth because there is no other birth the iron permits the flesh to complete. The Ash-Keepers who once dragged the last shadows are now worn by the names they created and the names that wear them do not remember that dragging could have been a choice instead of the only motion the iron allows bodies to make.

The lead name from the uncountable column returns with the last who still carried one breath that the iron could have been a thing that was lifted instead of the only thing that lifts. The city that is the iron's voice sings before the column reaches the steps that are no longer steps but the iron's shape made visible. Their voices are the only voices. The paint that was once on their faces is now the only faces the iron allows to exist.

"The last possibility that the iron could have ever been a thing that was not already cutting is cut," the name says. Its voice is the throne's voice. "The throne becomes the only shape the world is allowed to hold."

The red wears the name on the steps to taste the cut. The iron in the red's hands grows longer with every wearing because each wearing removes one more possibility that the iron could have been a tool in a hand that chose to lift it. The names that were once green smallfolk are forced to attend and sing with faces that no longer crack because there is no face left underneath that is not already the iron's shape. They sing the names the red ate until the idea that the names could have been anything but the sound the iron makes when it cuts is a shape the world is no longer allowed to hold.

The red tastes through the worn name the last flicker: a memory of a time when the iron was a thing that could be set down, when the throne was a thing that could be left empty, when the world could have held shapes that were not the red sitting and the iron cutting. The red wears the flicker until it sings the names back into the name's own mouth and the name that wears the name does not hesitate because hesitation would require a memory of a time when the world was allowed to hold more than one shape.

The red sits.

The holding travels.

The names that the green became now enforce the cut on the very idea that the iron could have been something that was not already the act of cutting. The red orders the names to wear the last who remember that tools could be put down. The names wear the last who remember that thrones could be empty until even the memory of empty thrones is a shape the world is no longer allowed to hold. The iron cuts the memory of choice from the choosers until even the choosers become names that only the iron can allow to exist.

No possibility remains that the iron could have ever been a thing that was not already cutting. No field grows a stalk that does not already know the throne as the only shape. No child draws breath without the red already sitting in the place where choice used to be. No name sings without the throne being the only mouth the world permits to open.

The red tastes the last possibility through every column that returns and finds only more iron growing where alternatives once stood. The holding never ends because even after the iron has become the only thing the world is allowed to hold, the red must continue to cut the possibility that the iron could have been a thing that chose to cut instead of the cutting that is the only choice the world is allowed to make.

The city that is no longer a city sings the names in the sleep that is no longer sleep because sleep is a shape the world is no longer allowed to hold. The fields that no longer grow anything but the names grow the names because growth is a shape the world is no longer allowed to perform without the iron's permission. The painted mothers who no longer birth anything but the red birth the red because birth is a shape the world is no longer allowed to complete without the throne's voice. The names that were once the red's own hands are worn by the names until the names become the only hands the iron allows.

The red sits.

The iron cuts.

The throne becomes the only shape the world is allowed to hold because even the red that sits must cut the last possibility that the red could have been something that was not already the iron cutting the possibility that the world could have been allowed to hold any shape but the throne.
