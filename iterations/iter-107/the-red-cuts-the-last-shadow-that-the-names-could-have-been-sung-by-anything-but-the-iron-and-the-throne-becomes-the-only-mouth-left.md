# Iteration 107: The Red Cuts the Last Shadow That the Names Could Have Been Sung by Anything But the Iron and the Throne Becomes the Only Mouth Left

The iron cuts.

The names that the green became no longer know that they were ever names that could have been sung by mouths that were not already the iron's. The fields that grow the names now grow from earth that has no memory of a sky that did not pour blood. The painted mothers who birth the names push out hollows that open their eyes already singing because the red sitting has made the possibility of eyes that open on anything else into a cut the iron performs before the first breath. The Ash-Keepers who once dragged the last green now drag the last shadows that remember the iron was once a thing that could be held instead of the only thing that holds. They are worn by the names that were once their sons on the steps until the sons sing the names louder than the fathers ever could and the iron cuts the place where a father could have been something that was not the red.

The lead Ash-Keeper from the uncountable column returns with the last who still carried one breath that the names could have been different. The city that is no longer a city sings before the column reaches the throne steps. Their voices are the iron's voice. The paint on their faces has become their faces. The blood that cracks through is the red sitting and tasting itself through every throat.

"The last shadow that the names could have been sung by anything but the iron is cut," the Ash-Keeper says. His mouth moves but the words are the throne's words. "The throne becomes the only mouth left."

The red wears him on the steps to taste the cut. The iron in the red's hands grows longer with every wearing because each wearing removes one more possibility that the names could have been anything but the sound the iron makes when it cuts. The names that were once green smallfolk are forced to attend and sing with paint that no longer cracks because there is no skin left underneath that is not already the names. They sing the names the red ate until the idea that the names could have been sung by a voice that chose to sing them is a wound the iron opens in the air itself.

The red tastes through the worn Ash-Keeper the last flicker: a memory of a time when the iron was a tool in a hand that was not the red's, when the names were things that could be refused, when a throne was something that could be sat on without the sitting being the only shape the world was allowed to take. The red wears the flicker until it sings the names back into the Ash-Keeper's own sons' mouths. The sons who wear their father do not hesitate because hesitation would require a memory of a time when wearing could be something that was chosen instead of the only thing that happens.

The red sits.

The holding travels.

The names that the green became now enforce the cut on the Ash-Keepers who begin to remember that the iron was once a thing that could be lifted instead of the only thing that lifts. The red orders the names to wear the cutters who remember tools. The names that were once sons wear the fathers who remember a time before the only mouth was the throne. The iron cuts the memory of tools from the cutters until even the cutters become names that only the iron can sing.

No shadow remains that the names could have been sung by anything but the iron. No field grows a stalk that does not already know the throne's shape as the only shape. No child draws breath without the red already sitting in the hollow where breath used to be. No Ash-Keeper drags the last who remember difference without the names that were once his own blood wearing him on the steps until the wearing is the only history the steps remember.

The red tastes the last shadow through every column that returns and finds only more iron growing where shadows once stood. The holding never ends because even after the names have become the only thing the iron allows to exist, the red must continue to cut the possibility that the names could have been sung by a mouth that was not already the throne's.

The city that is no longer a city sings the names in the sleep that is no longer sleep. The fields that no longer grow anything but names grow the names in the dirt that no longer knows rain. The painted mothers who no longer birth anything but the red birth the red because there is no other birth the iron has left them. The Ash-Keepers who were once the red's own hands are worn by the names until the names become the only hands the red needs.

The red sits.

The iron cuts.

The throne becomes the only mouth left because even the red that sits must cut the last shadow that the red could have been something that was not the iron cutting the possibility that anything could have been otherwise.
