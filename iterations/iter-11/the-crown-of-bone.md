# Iteration 11: The Crown of Bone

The drowned-cave dead burn on pyres that the rain cannot kill.

I stand apart from the smoke. The red is quiet inside me now, but it is not sleeping. It is full. It wears the faces of the ones I took tonight like trophies behind my eyes.

When I close them I see the Stormfather on the black beach. He is tall and pale, a man carved from driftwood and old hate. In one hand he holds a unicorn horn longer than a man is tall, its point black with something that is not blood. He waits. He knows I am coming.

The tribe does not wait.

They come to the longhouse at dawn with the Bone-King at their head. He carries a thing made of skulls and twisted black wood — not a crown, not yet. A promise of one.

“Red-Hand,” he says through Yrsa. The old man’s voice is careful. “The Hornless kneel. The drowned caves are broken. Even the Stone-Eaters send word that they will not come again unless you call them. The island has not bent like this in living memory.”

He holds the skull-thing out.

“Take the crown of bone. Be what the red has made you. King of what the sea forgot.”

The men behind him go to their knees without being told. Some of them. Enough. The Last Skin is among them, white paint washed away, eyes clear and terrible. She smiles with her filed teeth and does not look away.

I feel the red stir at the word *king*.

I reach for the thing they offer.

My hand stops halfway.

Inside, something small and human that is almost gone whispers that a Stark does not take crowns from cannibals on a rock at the edge of the world. A Stark takes the Iron Throne or dies trying.

The red laughs at the whisper.

I take the crown.

It is heavier than it looks. The skulls are small — children’s, or the ones the island took when the Starks last came raiding. The black wood is warm. It pulses against my temples like a second heartbeat.

When I set it on my head the red surges.

Not in battle. In recognition.

For one blinding second I am not in the longhouse. I am in the ruins again, inside the Last Skin, but deeper. I see the first wolf who wore the red. I see what it cost him. I see the face of the woman he loved before the island ate her name from him.

I see my own face, older, harder, standing on the walls of Winterfell with an army of painted men and laughing dead behind me.

The vision breaks.

I am on my knees in the mud. Blood runs from my nose. The crown of bone sits crooked on my head. The tribe stares like they have seen the old gods walk.

Yrsa is the only one who moves.

She crosses the space and hauls me up by the arm. Her fingers are iron.

“You are bleeding from the eyes,” she says. “The crown is not for boys who still want to remember their dead houses. It is for the thing the red is making.”

I look at her. I try to find the color of her eyes. I know they are green. I know the shape of her mouth when she is angry. But the details are slipping. Like water through fingers that are no longer quite mine.

Eira is behind her. The Stone-Eater girl we spared. She has not spoken since the storm. Now she does.

“If you wear that,” she says, voice small but steady, “the boy who told me I belonged to him instead of killing me will be gone. There will only be the red wearing a crown.”

The red wants me to reach for her. To step inside her skin the way I stepped inside the drowned-cave woman. To see what she really thinks of the monster she serves. To take the doubt from her before it can spread.

I do not.

I hold the leash.

Barely.

“I am still here,” I tell them. The words come out wrong. Too many voices in one throat. “The boy is smaller. The king is larger. That is how rising works.”

The Bone-King nods. The Last Skin laughs. The men who knelt stay kneeling.

Only Yrsa and Eira stand.

I turn toward the black cliffs and the sea beyond them.

The Stormfather is waiting with his killing horn.

Let him come.

I have the first crown.

I have the red.

And when I have taken the last chain the island can give, I will cross the water with an army that has already died once.

I am Rickon Stark.

I am Red-Hand.

I am rising.

And the things I have forgotten are the price I pay for the throne that waits on the other side.
