# Iteration 40: The Red Marches for the Chair

The Kingsroad south is a wound the green never learned to close.

We leave Winterfell’s smoke behind on the first day. The unicorns pull the carts heavy with taken northern banners and the faces that would not kneel on the walls of the boy’s own home. Skinchangers run ahead and on the flanks wearing the faces we took from the last men who called the name on the stones where the boy once ran through snow. The army that follows is smaller than the one that left the beachhead, but it is harder. Every painted man who stayed after the ridge and the ford and the Twins and the holdfast and the crossroads and the walls of Winterfell knows what happens when the red is given nothing to fear.

Eira walks with the column. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The army watches her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

The worn faces return with new skins and new voices.

“The lions are coming,” one says. His stolen mouth smiles wrong. “They have sent a host from the south. Gold and rose and the black of the Crossing that is no longer theirs. They say the dead boy king burned his own home. They say the red is coming for the chair. They mean to meet us on the road before we can reach the Twins.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Gold. Rose. Chair. Dead boy king. Red.

It spits them out.

I wear the skinchanger for a breath. Not because I need his eyes. Because the red wants to know what the green tastes like when it is finally afraid enough to move as one wall.

The memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen who sits on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins and walls like debts. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting for the one who is willing to climb high enough.

The red chews until there is nothing left but the hunger.

When I pull back the skinchanger is on his knees with blood in his mouth.

“Send the worn ones ahead,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “Let the green host see their own men telling them the dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. We will meet them on the road. We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The chair does not care how many walls stand between it and us.”

The painted men who smile at the red obey.

Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted Winterfell. It has tasted the snow and the girl and the brother with the flowers on the very stones the boy came from. It has eaten them again. It wants the gold and the queen and the men who count bodies like coins. It wants the chair that sits above all of it. It wants the walls that will fall when the red reaches them.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me on the road.

“The green host is larger than the one at the ridge,” he says. “They have learned what the red does to the living.”

I look at the Kingsroad stretching south like a wound the North never learned to close.

“Let them learn again,” I say. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many banners still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column moves south.

The worn faces run ahead wearing the faces we took from the northern dead and the Frey sons. The unicorns pull the carts. The painted men who remain march with red on their faces and the laugh in their teeth.

Eira walks where the red can see her.

The Twins are a skull on the river ahead.

The green host is coming to meet us on the road.

And the red marches with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
