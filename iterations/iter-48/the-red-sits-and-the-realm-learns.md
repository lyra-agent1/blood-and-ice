# Iteration 48: The Red Sits and the Realm Learns

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red does not mind the bite. The hollow is full of the climb. The climb is done. The chair is cold and sharp and right. It fits the shape of what remains.

The painted men kneel in the throne room. Red faces. Faces of the dead on their shoulders. They stack the last of the green dead the Skagosi way along the walls. The banners of the lion and the rose and the black that is no longer black burn in the braziers. The city outside the Red Keep still smokes where the worn ones finished what the breach began.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and the last chain that once called itself Eira and the white knight who tried to stop the red on the city wall are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both.

A painted man steps forward. One of the old Skagosi. His face is red with ash and the blood of the last host. He does not lower his eyes the way the green once did.

“The lords will come,” he says. “Some will kneel. Some will send men. The city is yours but the realm still has teeth.”

The red tastes the words.

Lords. Kneel. Men. Teeth.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold what was climbed.

“Send the worn ones,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Let every lord see his own man walking his halls with the red behind his eyes. Let them hear the message from the chair. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The chair does not care how many lords still fly the old colors. Only that they kneel or burn.”

The painted man smiles with red in his teeth. He knows the shape of the message now.

I do not rise.

The swords dig deeper when I shift. The red welcomes it. Pain is just another wall that was climbed and now must be held. The hollow does not rest. The red does not rest. The chair does not rest.

A skinchanger returns wearing a face that smells of the riverlands and old oaths. He kneels at the foot of the throne.

“Lord of the Crossing is dead,” he says with a dead man’s mouth. “The Twins burn. The lords of the North send no word. They remember the red on the walls of Winterfell. They remember the lost wolf who burned his own home.”

The red tastes the words through the stolen mouth.

North. Winterfell. Lost wolf. Burned home.

It has eaten these already. It eats them again.

“Send more worn ones north,” I say. “Let the North see its own dead walking its halls. Let them hear the message from the chair. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. Kneel or the red will come for what remains of your halls.”

The skinchanger’s body empties on the stone when I pull back. The painted men take it without word. They have learned what to do with the empty ones.

Another returns wearing gold.

“The queen is dead,” he says. “The lions that remain hide behind their walls or send terms. They say the red may keep the North and the Crossing and the cursed seat and the city. They say the chair belongs to the one who can hold it. They ask what the red will do with the realm it has taken.”

The red tastes the words through the golden mouth.

Queen. Dead. Lions. Terms. Hold it. Realm.

It chews until nothing remains but the climb that is now the holding.

“Send the worn ones with the answer,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It carries across the throne room like wind through bone. “The red does not bargain. The red does not share. The chair does not care how many lions still sit on gold. Only that they kneel or the red will wear what they send and stack what they leave. The rising does not end at the city. The red holds what it climbed.”

The golden body empties. The painted men take it. They have learned the shape of the answer.

I sit.

The iron cuts.

The red does not mind.

A painted man — younger, one who came from the island after the Bone-King fell — steps closer than the others dare. His face is red with fresh paint. His eyes are the red’s eyes now.

“What do we do with the city?” he asks. “What do we do with the realm?”

The red looks at him through the hollow.

The red looks at the chair made of swords.

“We hold it,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “We send the worn ones to every hall. We wear what will not kneel. We stack what will not burn. We teach the realm the shape of the red that climbed. The chair does not care how many kings sat here before. Only that we sit now. Only that we hold.”

The painted man smiles with red in his teeth. He kneels.

Not because he remembers a boy.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to climb and how to hold what was climbed.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

The iron chair cuts.

The red sits.

The realm learns.

The worn ones run.

The painted men stack.

The city burns red where it must.

And the red on the throne made of a thousand swords waits for what the green will send next.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding.
