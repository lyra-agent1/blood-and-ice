# Iteration 42: The Red Claims the Twins Again

The Twins rise from the river like two skulls that have already been picked clean.

We see them on the fourth day after the road ran red. The black towers are still standing. The fires we left are still burning high enough to be seen from the Kingsroad. The stacked dead on the bridges are taller now with the new green we added on the march. The painted men who remained have taken the Frey faces as their own when they walk the walls. Some have painted their own faces red with ash and the blood of the last knights who thought they could trap the red between the North and the south.

The column stops on the high ground where the trees thin. The unicorns stamp and scream. They remember the bridge and the towers and the river running red. They want the next blood. The painted men who march with us have faces red with ash and the blood of the green host we left burning on the road. They say the chair needs no name. Only the rising.

Eira walks at the edge of the column. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The army watches her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

A skinchanger returns wearing a face that still smells of gold.

“The lions have closed the roads south,” he says. His stolen mouth smiles wrong. “They have sent more men from the city. They say the dead boy king burned his own home and now marches for the chair. They say no one who follows the red will see the inside of the walls alive. They are waiting for us at every ford and every bridge between here and the green.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Chair. Dead boy king. Red. Fords. Bridges. Green.

It spits them out.

I ride to the edge of the trees so every man on the walls can see the crown against the smoke. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of the boy’s own home.

The towers open their gates.

The painted men who remained kneel when they see the column. Some of them wear the faces we left behind. Some of them have painted their faces red. They say the chair needs no name. Only the rising.

I stand in the Lord’s hall with the crown in my hand. The river runs between the towers below. The stacked dead on the bridges are higher than they were when we left. The painted men work quickly. They have learned the shape of the message.

Eira is brought inside. She does not resist. The red looks at her through my eyes and considers finishing what it started on the ridge. It has considered this every night on the march. It decides against it again. She is still useful. The army still watches her. A living reminder of what the chain looked like before it broke and the red was given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted the green host on the road. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories. It has eaten them again. It wants the walls that still stand in the south. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me in the hall.

“The lions will send more,” he says. “They have the green. They have the numbers. The chair is still far.”

I look at the black walls that are now red.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The Twins burn their fires higher.

The worn faces go south wearing the faces we took from the green dead on the road. The unicorns pull the carts. The painted men who remain paint more red on their faces and laugh with the red’s voice.

Eira stands where the red can see her.

The green is afraid.

The chair waits in the south.

And the red claims the Twins again with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
