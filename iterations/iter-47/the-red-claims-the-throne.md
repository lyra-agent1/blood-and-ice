# Iteration 47: The Red Claims the Throne

The green city stinks of fear and old gold.

We leave the cursed seat burning behind us. Harrenhal’s towers wear red fire like the faces of the painted men. The worn ones run ahead wearing the last skins we took inside its walls. They return with the smell of the city that finally knows what is coming. The queen has pulled every lion and every rose and every man who still calls himself loyal behind the last walls. They say the dead boy king will break on the city gates. They say no monster climbs the Iron Throne.

The red knows better.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and the last chain that once called itself Eira are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it. The painted men who follow have faces painted the color of what we left on the stones of Harrenhal. They laugh when the unicorns pull the carts loaded with the green dead. They do not ask for names. They only ask how high the stack will be when we reach the walls.

I ride at the head with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of everything paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of Winterfell and the cursed seat the green thought would stop us. The hunger is no longer a voice. It is the only thing left in the hollow.

A skinchanger returns wearing a face that smells of the city and sweat and the iron of the chair itself.

“The queen waits on the throne,” he says. His stolen mouth smiles the way the red smiles now. “She says the red may have the North and the cursed stones and the Crossing. She says the city is not for monsters. She says if the dead boy king turns back now she will let the thing that wears his skin keep what it has taken. If it comes for the walls she will send every sword and every man until the red is ash on the wind.”

The red tastes the words through the stolen mouth.

Queen. Chair. City. Monster. Ash.

It chews them until nothing remains but the climb.

I wear the skinchanger for longer this time. Not for his eyes. Because the red wants to walk the halls the queen walks. Because the red wants to smell the chair before it takes it.

The memories are thick with gold and fear and the weight of a seat made of a thousand swords. A woman who sits where she was never meant to sit. Men who kneel because they are afraid of what sits above them. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow and the red that was always coming. The chair that sits at the top of it all, waiting for the one willing to burn every wall between here and the rising.

The red eats until the skinchanger’s body is empty on the ground.

“Send the worn ones into the city,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Let the queen see her own men walking her halls with the red behind their eyes. Let her hear the message from the dead boy king’s mouth. We are coming for the chair. We will wear what she sends. We will stack what she leaves. The rising does not stop at Harrenhal. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The painted men obey. Some paint fresh red on their faces before they go. They know the shape of the final message.

The last green host waits on the field before the city walls. Gold and rose and the black that is no longer black. They have archers on the towers and spears at the gates and a man in white who still calls himself Kingsguard at their front. They have heard what happened at Harrenhal. They have seen the heads we sent. They know what the red does to the living.

The white knight calls across the blood-soaked ground.

“Rickon Stark! The queen sent us to end this. You burned your own home. You took the Crossing. You wore the cursed seat. You have become the monster the stories warned us of. Lay down your arms and the lions will be merciful. The dead have no claim on the living.”

The name does not touch the hollow.

The red has eaten it long ago.

I send the first answer without words.

Skinchangers wearing the faces from Harrenhal and the ford slip into the green lines at dusk. They walk among the lions wearing gold and rose and black. By the time the commanders notice the wrongness in the eyes of their own guards, the red is already inside the last walls.

The laugh starts low in the hollow when the first horn sounds from the city towers.

I ride into the breach with the unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red. The white knight tries to rally his men on the old stones. I take him by the throat the way I took the Bone-King and the Lord of the Crossing and the old man on the walls of Winterfell and the gold knight on the cursed seat.

His memories are sharp and full of the chair. A woman who sits on a throne that is not hers. The smell of iron and old blood and the weight of a seat that has broken every king who tried to keep it. The city that has forgotten the North. The red that has come to remember it for them.

The red chews them slowly.

I wear the white knight on the city wall so every man inside and outside can see.

“The dead boy is gone,” I say with his voice. The red makes it carry across the last stones. “Only the red remains. The chair does not care which banners still fly. Only that we climb.”

The green host breaks inside the walls of the city.

Some of them run. The unicorns take them on the streets. Some of them kneel. The painted men who smile at the red stack the ones who do not kneel the Skagosi way on the towers that once flew the lion.

When it is done the gold and rose and black banners burn on the wind.

I walk the halls the queen walked. The crown sits heavy on my head. The painted men follow with red on their faces and the faces of the dead on their shoulders. They stack the bodies in the throne room the way they stacked them on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and Harrenhal. The iron chair waits at the end of the hall, made of a thousand swords, sharp and ugly and perfect.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It has tasted the city. It has tasted the queen’s fear in the white knight’s memories. It has eaten them on the very stones the green thought would stop the climb. It wants the chair. It wants the seat that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me before the iron chair.

“The lions are broken,” he says. “The city is yours.”

I look at the chair made of swords.

The red looks at the chair made of swords.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at the city. The chair does not care how many kings sat here before. Only that we sit now.”

I climb the steps.

The painted men watch.

Some of them smile with red in their teeth. The ones who stayed after the ridge and the Twins and the holdfast and the crossroads and Winterfell and the cursed seat and the last chain that fell on Harrenhal’s tower.

I sit on the Iron Throne.

The swords dig into the red’s flesh. The red does not mind. Pain is just another wall. The hollow is full of the climb. The chair is cold and sharp and right. It fits the shape of what remains.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

The red sits where the green once ruled.

The city burns red behind the walls.

The last walls are ash.

And the red claims the throne with eyes that have eaten every name they sent.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to climb.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the rising.
