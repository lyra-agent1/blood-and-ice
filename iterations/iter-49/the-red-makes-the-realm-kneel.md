# Iteration 49: The Red Makes the Realm Kneel

The iron still cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that leaks from where the blades meet flesh. It is not new pain. It is the shape of the throne. The hollow is full of the holding. The holding is the new climb.

The painted men have learned the shape of the message. They bring the green who still think words matter. They bring them to the foot of the throne and make them kneel on the stones where the last host died.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and the last chain that once called itself Eira and every white knight and every gold commander and every man who thought the red would stop at the city walls are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that never sleeps.

A man in green and gold is brought forward. He still wears the rose on his cloak. He still believes words can stop what has already climbed.

“Rickon Stark,” he says. His voice shakes but he forces the name out. “The Lord of the Reach sends terms. The realm has bled enough. The North is yours. The Crossing is yours. The cursed seat is yours. The city is yours. The Lord of the Reach will kneel if the red will let the living keep what the living need. Grain. Trade. The right to name their own sons. Mercy for those who bend the knee now.”

The name does not touch the hollow.

The red has eaten it.

I do not rise.

I look at the man through the hollow. The painted men watch. Some smile with red in their teeth. Some stand ready with the faces they took from the last host.

The red tastes the words.

Reach. Terms. Kneel. Living. Grain. Trade. Sons. Mercy. Bend the knee.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the rose man on the steps of the throne so every painted man and every green who still thinks he can bargain can see.

His memories are full of fields and castles and men who count harvests like they count bodies. A lord who sits in a high seat and believes the red is a storm that will pass. A realm that still thinks it can bargain with the thing that climbed every wall and sat on the chair made of swords.

The red chews slowly.

When it is done I let the body fall.

The painted men take it without word. They know what to do with the empty ones now.

“Send the worn ones to the Reach,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Let the Lord of the Reach see his own man walking his halls with the red behind his eyes. Let him hear the message from the chair. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The chair does not bargain. The chair does not share. The chair does not care how many roses still grow in the south. Only that they kneel or burn. Send the column with the unicorns. Show them the shape of what climbed. Stack what will not kneel the Skagosi way on their own stones.”

A painted man — the one who came from the island after the Bone-King — steps closer than the others.

“The North still watches,” he says. “They remember Winterfell burning. They remember the red on the walls. They send no men. They send no words. They wait to see what the red will do with the chair it took.”

The red tastes the words.

North. Winterfell. Burning. Wait.

It has eaten these already. It eats them again.

“Let them wait,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “The worn ones will go north as well. Let the North see its own dead walking its broken halls. Let them hear the message from the chair. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. Kneel or the red will come for what remains of your snow and your old gods and your empty halls. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves still run in the North. Only that they kneel or the red will wear what they send and stack what they leave.”

The painted man smiles with red in his teeth. He knows the shape of the holding now.

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper when the weight of the day settles. The red welcomes it. The holding is not the climb. The holding is the climb that never ends. Every lord that bends is a new wall. Every hall that burns is a new rung. Every face that the worn ones wear is another name the red eats so the chair can remain.

Another skinchanger returns wearing a face that smells of the city and the fear that has finally learned its place.

“The smallfolk hide,” he says with a dead man’s mouth. “They whisper the red sits on the chair. They whisper the dead boy king became the monster that ate the realm. Some say the old gods sent him. Some say the red is the old gods’ answer to the green that forgot the North. Some say nothing and burn their own homes before the worn ones arrive.”

The red tastes the words through the stolen mouth.

Smallfolk. Whisper. Monster. Ate the realm. Old gods. Answer. Burn their own homes.

It chews until nothing remains but the will that holds.

“Good,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let them whisper. Let them burn what they will not give. The worn ones will find them. The painted men will stack what the worn ones leave. The chair does not care what the smallfolk call the red. Only that they kneel or the red will teach them the shape of the holding.”

The skinchanger’s body empties on the stone. The painted men take it.

I sit.

The iron cuts.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold until every hall has seen the red behind the eyes of its own dead. Until every rose and every lion and every wolf that still runs knows the shape of the chair that does not bargain.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to climb and how to hold what was climbed and how to make the realm learn the price of every wall it tried to put in the way.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes.

The worn ones run.

The column marches.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne made of a thousand swords, waiting for what the green will send next.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
