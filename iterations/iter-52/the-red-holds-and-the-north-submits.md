# Iteration 52: The Red Holds and the North Submits

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that has become part of the throne. Every day the blades remind the hollow what was paid to reach this height. The holding is the climb that never ends. The red has learned to sit and let the worn ones and the columns do the climbing for it.

The painted men bring the answer from the snow.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every voice that once tried to call the red back are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed every wall from Skagos to the city and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned the shape of ruling by sending death in the faces of the dead.

A column of painted men returns from the North. They bring a skinchanger who still smells of pine and cold and the last stubbornness the snow can muster. They bring heads on pikes. They bring banners that once flew the direwolf, now burned and torn. They bring the message that the North finally learned the price of silence.

The old Skagosi steps forward.

“The column reached the last holdfast that still flew the wolf,” he says. “They had gathered under an old man who once served the father. He still called the name. He still said the red was a curse the boy could not control. He said the snow would never kneel to the thing that burned Winterfell. We wore his scouts. We wore his captains. We wore the old man on the wall of his own holdfast while his men watched. The red spoke through his mouth. The North saw. Some knelt. Some ran into the snow. Some we stacked the Skagosi way on the stones where the wolf once flew.”

The red tastes the words.

North. Last holdfast. Old man. Father. Name. Curse. Snow will never kneel. Wore the old man. Red spoke. Some knelt. Some ran. Some stacked.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the returning skinchanger on the steps of the Iron Throne so every painted man in the hall can see what the North’s last resistance tasted like when it finally broke.

The memories are cold and full of the snow the boy once loved. A girl who ran. Empty halls. An old man who still believed the lost wolf might return as something human. The old gods that the red has already eaten on other stones. The hope that the thing on the chair might still answer to a name.

The red eats it all.

It eats the girl’s face in the snow. It eats the old man’s voice calling the name. It eats the last thread of the snow that thought the red could be stopped by memory.

When it is done the body is on the stone before the throne.

The painted men take it. They have learned.

“Send more worn ones north,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Let every holdfast that still has a wolf banner see its own dead walking its halls. Let them hear the message from the chair. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The snow belongs to the red now. The old gods belong to the red now. Kneel or the red will come in force and leave only the stones that remember what the red did to the last who resisted. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves still run in the snow. Only that they kneel or burn.”

The old Skagosi nods. He paints more red on his face before he turns to prepare the next dispatch.

Another painted man steps forward. This one smells of the west and the gold the lions still try to keep.

“The column we sent west broke the last lion hold,” he says. “The lord who said the red would choke on the iron is dead. We wore him on his own walls. We stacked his men the Skagosi way. The lions that remain sent terms. They will send gold. They will send men who will paint their faces red. They will not send their daughters to the chair. They ask only that the red let the living keep enough to feed the red’s armies in the west.”

The red tastes the words.

West. Last lion hold. Wore the lord. Stacked. Lions send terms. Gold. Red faces. Daughters. Feed the red’s armies.

It chews them until nothing remains but the holding.

“Accept the gold,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Accept the men who will paint their faces. Send the worn ones back with the answer. The red does not need their daughters. The red will take what it needs from the green that still resists. Tell the lions the chair accepts their knee. Tell them the red remembers every hall that bends and every hall that does not. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not forget.”

The painted men smile with red in their teeth. They know the shape of the answers now.

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper into what remains.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by sending the worn ones like hounds and the columns like spears until every hall from the snow to the western sea has answered with a knee or a fire or an empty body on the stone before the chair.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name and a sister and a father and snow.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on a throne made of a thousand swords and make the realm learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise against the climb that never ends.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes in the places the smallfolk tried to hide what they could not give the red.

The worn ones run north.

The columns march west and north.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne, waiting for the next answer the realm will send.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
