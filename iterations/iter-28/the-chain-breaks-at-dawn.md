# Iteration 28: The Chain Breaks at Dawn

The sun finds the ridge already red.

The green host comes in the old way. Shields locked. Banners high. Rose and lion and the black of the Crossing moving as one wall up the slope toward the Stark stones. Two thousand men who have heard the stories and decided the dead boy must die again before the sun sets.

I stand on the broken wall. The crown is lighter now. One horn piece remains. Yrsa holds the spear at my side. Eira stands with the last piece in her hand, blood dried on her face from the night. The army behind us is no longer one thing. Some painted men have knives ready for the anchors. Some have knives ready for the green. Most wait to see which red will win.

Inside the hollow there is almost no boy left.

Only the chair. Only the rising. Only the red waiting to be fed.

The first arrows come with the light.

Unicorns scream and charge down the slope. Skinchangers run with them, wearing the faces we took in the night. I go with them. The red goes with me.

Not all the way. Not yet.

We meet the green shield wall on the lower slope. The laugh starts in my chest before the first sword finds me. It is not my laugh. It has not been mine for a long time.

A spear takes me in the side. The red laughs louder. I pull the man who threw it into the hollow and wear him before his body hits the ground. Then I turn him on the men who stood beside him.

The shield wall breaks.

The green sends more. They have numbers. They have the will of men who believe they fight a monster that must be put down. They are not wrong.

Yrsa holds the line on the stones above. Eira calls the skinchangers back when they start to wear too deep. The red wants them. It wants all of them.

A knight in rose and gold breaks through the painted men and comes for the wall. He calls my name as he climbs.

“Rickon Stark! The North sent us to bring you home!”

The name hits the hollow like a hammer.

There is nothing left for it to eat but the last scraps. The red takes them anyway. The snow. The girl. The brother. The father. The name itself. It chews and spits the pieces into the chair and keeps laughing.

I meet the knight on the broken steps.

The red takes him.

I wear him in front of his own men. I make him kill three before I let him fall. The army on the ridge sees their king laughing with a green man’s mouth while the last horn piece still hangs from the crown.

Some of the painted men cheer.

Some of them turn their knives toward Yrsa and Eira.

The red surges.

It is done waiting.

It wants the last anchor. It wants to walk the ridge wearing Eira’s face and speak with her voice and make the green understand that there is no boy left to save. It wants the chair and it wants the world to burn until the chair is the only thing left standing.

Yrsa plants her spear in front of Eira.

The painted men who want the chain cut come anyway.

I do not stop them.

I let the red go.

The laugh that comes out of me is the one the Skagosi feared on the island. The one that made the Bone-King yield. The one that turned the river red. It is louder now. It has been fed on names and brothers and girls who laughed in the snow.

I take the first painted man who reaches Eira and wear him. Then another. Then I stop wearing and simply kill with my own hands because the red no longer needs the mask.

The knight’s banner falls. The rose and lion break. The black of the Crossing tries to hold and fails when their own lord turns on them with a skinchanger’s eyes.

Yrsa falls with a spear in her back while she holds the line for Eira. She does not cry out. She plants the spear in the stones one last time and looks at me with the old Skagosi eyes.

“Say it,” she says with blood in her mouth. “Even now.”

There is nothing left to say.

The red takes her face for a moment. Not to wear. Just to taste what loyalty tasted like before it was eaten.

Eira is the last.

She stands on the wall with the final horn piece raised like a knife. The painted men who survived the charge circle her. The green who are still alive on the slope watch their king become the thing they came to kill.

I step toward her.

The red is already inside my mouth.

She throws the last horn piece at my feet.

“Take it,” she says. “If you are still in there. Take the last chain and finish what you started. Or let the red have us all and see what the chair is worth when there is no one left to sit on it.”

The red laughs.

It takes her anyway.

Not to wear for long. Just long enough to make the green herald who still lives carry the message down the slope. Long enough for the painted men who wanted the chain cut to see what freedom from it looks like.

When I pull back she is on her knees. Alive. Barely. The horn piece is in my hand.

The green host is breaking.

The ones who can run are running. The ones who cannot are stacked the Skagosi way on the slope below the tower. The unicorns feast. The skinchangers who remain wear the faces of the dead and laugh with the red’s voice.

I stand on the Stark stones with the crown in my hand.

No horns left on it.

No anchors left.

The army that remains is smaller. Some fled when the red took Eira. Some stayed because they have seen what happens when the chain breaks and they would rather follow the monster than die on the green’s spears.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The girl who laughed is gone. The brother with the flowers is gone. The father and the snow and the old gods are gone.

Only the rising remains.

“Burn the dead,” I say. The voice that comes out is not mine. It is the red’s now. “Stack them high. Let the green see what waits for them at every tower between here and the walls.”

The men who kneel do it without hesitation.

The ones who do not kneel are already dead or gone.

I set the crown back on my head. It is light without the horns.

The chair waits.

The green is afraid.

And the red is hungry.

We march at first light.
