# Iteration 43: The Red Sends the Message

The Twins are a skull we have already picked clean.

We hold them for two nights while the worn faces run south and return with new skins and the smell of gold. They say the lions have closed every road between here and the city. They have put men at every ford and every bridge. They have sent a host larger than the one we broke on the Kingsroad. Gold and rose and the black that is no longer black. They say the dead boy king will not pass. They say the red will burn itself out before it reaches the chair.

I stand in the Lord’s hall with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of Winterfell. The painted men who remain have faces red with ash and the blood of the green we left on the road. They laugh with the red’s voice when they drink. They say the chair needs no name. Only the rising.

Eira stands where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The army watches her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

I send the message without words first.

Skinchangers wearing the faces we took from the green host on the road slip south at dusk. They walk among the lions’ men wearing gold and rose and black. By the time the commanders notice the wrongness in the eyes of their own scouts, the red is already inside their lines.

Then I send the worn ones who still have tongues.

They carry the heads of the knights we broke on the Kingsroad. They carry the direwolf banners we burned at Winterfell. They carry the message the red has taught the green and the black and now the gold.

The dead boy king is gone.

Only the red remains.

We are coming for the chair.

The painted men who smile at the red cheer when the worn ones leave. Some of them paint more red on their faces before they go. They have seen what happens when the chain is cut and the red is given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not angry.

It is hungry.

It has tasted the green host on the road. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories. It has eaten them again. It wants the walls that still stand in the south. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me in the hall.

“The lions will not break easy,” he says. “They have the numbers. They have the green.”

I look at the black walls that are now red.

“Let them have the numbers,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The Twins burn their fires higher.

The worn faces run south wearing the faces we took from the green dead.

The unicorns pull the carts.

The painted men who remain paint more red on their faces and laugh with the red’s voice.

Eira stands where the red can see her.

The green is afraid.

The chair waits in the south.

And the red sends the message with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
