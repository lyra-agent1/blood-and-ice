# Iteration 45: The Red Claims the Cursed Seat

The green heartlands smell of blood and old fires.

We cross the last ford on the second day after the river ran red. The worn faces run ahead wearing gold and rose and black. They return with new skins and the smell of a city that has finally remembered the North and the boy who once ran through snow. They say the lions have pulled back to the great cursed seat. Harrenhal. The place the green calls haunted. The place that has swallowed armies and kings and left only bones and ash. They say the dead boy king will break himself against its walls. They say the red will drown in its own hunger before it reaches the chair.

I ride at the head of the column with the crown on my head. No horns. No chains. The light shape of what was paid for on Skagos and the ridge and the Twins and the Kingsroad and the walls of Winterfell. The unicorns pull the carts with the taken banners and the faces that would not kneel. Skinchangers run ahead wearing the faces we took from the green dead at the ford.

Eira walks where the red can see her. She does not speak. She carries what the red left her and keeps the pace. The painted men who remain have faces red with ash and the blood of the knights we broke on the road. They watch her the way men watch a storm that has already broken and might break again. Some smile with red in their teeth. None of them look away for long.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will to climb and the red that feeds it.

Harrenhal rises from the green like a corpse that has refused to rot.

We see it on the third day after the ford. Massive. Broken. Black stone that has seen too many fires and too many dead. The green host waits in its shadow — gold and rose and the black that is no longer black. They have archers on the broken towers and spears in the ruined gate and a man in gold who still calls himself Lannister at the front. They have heard the message we sent from the Twins. They have seen the heads and the burned banners. They know what the red does to the living.

The knight calls across the broken ground.

“Rickon Stark! The queen sent us to end this. You burned your own home. You took the Crossing. You have become the monster the stories warned us of. Lay down your arms and the lions will be merciful. The dead have no claim on the living.”

The name does not touch the hollow.

The red has eaten it.

I send the first answer without words.

Skinchangers wearing the faces from the ford and the Twins slip into the ruined walls at dusk. They walk among the green men wearing gold and rose and black. By the time the commanders notice the wrongness in the eyes of their own guards, the red is already inside the cursed seat.

The laugh starts low in the hollow when the first horn sounds from the broken towers.

I ride into Harrenhal with the unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red. The green knight tries to rally his men on the old stones. I take him by the throat the way I took the Bone-King and the Lord of the Crossing and the old man on the walls of Winterfell.

His memories are sharp and full of gold. A queen who sits on a throne that is not hers. Men who count bodies like coins. The smell of a city that has forgotten the North and the boy who once ran through snow. The chair that sits above all of it, waiting for the one who is willing to climb high enough.

The red chews them slowly.

I wear the knight on the broken wall so every man inside and outside can see.

“The dead boy is gone,” I say with his voice. The red makes it carry across the cursed stones. “Only the red remains. The chair does not care which banners still fly. Only that we climb.”

The green host breaks inside the walls of Harrenhal.

Some of them run. The unicorns take them on the old stones. Some of them kneel. The painted men who smile at the red stack the ones who do not kneel the Skagosi way on the broken towers.

When it is done the gold and rose and black banners are ash on the wind.

I stand on the broken wall of Harrenhal with the crown in my hand. The stacked dead are higher than they were at the Twins. The painted men work quickly. They have learned the shape of the message.

Eira is brought inside the cursed seat. She does not resist. The red looks at her through my eyes and considers finishing what it started on the ridge. It has considered this every night since the Twins. The hunger is closer now. The chair is close enough to taste. It decides against it again. She is still useful. The army still watches her. A living reminder of what the chain looked like before it broke and the red was given nothing to fear.

Inside the hollow the red is not satisfied.

It has tasted Harrenhal. It has tasted the gold and the queen and the chair in the knight’s memories. It has eaten them again on the very stones the green calls haunted. It wants the walls that still stand between here and the city. It wants the throne that sits above all of them. It wants the city that has forgotten the name the red ate.

A painted man — one of the old Skagosi who came from the island — steps beside me on the broken wall.

“The lions will send more,” he says. “They have the green. They have the city.”

I look at the cursed stones that are now red.

“Let them send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through a skull. “We will wear what they send. We will stack what they leave. The rising does not stop at Winterfell. The chair does not care how many walls still fly the old colors or how many lions still sit on gold. Only that we climb.”

The column makes Harrenhal its own.

Fires burn high on the broken towers.

The green is afraid.

The chair waits in the south.

And the red claims the cursed seat with eyes that have already eaten every name they sent.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It climbs.
