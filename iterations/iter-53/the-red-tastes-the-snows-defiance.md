# Iteration 53: The Red Tastes the Snow's Defiance

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that has become the throne’s own. Every day the blades dig deeper. Every day the hollow learns that the climb does not end when the chair is taken. The holding is the climb that never stops. The red sits and sends the worn ones and the columns to do the work of the rising.

The painted men bring the answer from the snow.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every voice that once tried to pull the red back into a body that could feel the cold are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed from Skagos to the city and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned to sit and let the realm bleed for it.

A column returns from the North. The painted men who went with the unicorns and the worn faces come back with fewer than left. They bring heads. They bring torn direwolf banners. They bring a skinchanger who smells of pine and blood and something that still fights the red even after the wearing.

The old Skagosi steps forward. His face is red with fresh paint and the blood of the men he stacked on the way back.

“The snow fights,” he says. “We reached the holdfasts that still flew the wolf. We wore their scouts. We wore their captains. We wore the old man on his own wall and left the message. Some knelt. Some we stacked. But the snow itself pushed back. The old gods moved in the trees. Skinchangers loyal to the North wore the faces we sent. They turned our own worn ones against us. Men who painted their faces red woke with the red’s voice in their mouths and the North’s knives in their backs. The land remembers the boy who ran. The snow does not want the red. The old gods do not want the red. We lost men. We lost faces. The North is not like the green. The North bites back.”

The red tastes the words.

Snow. Fights. Old gods. Trees. Skinchangers loyal to the North. Wore the faces we sent. Turned our own against us. Land remembers the boy. Snow does not want the red. Old gods do not want the red. Lost men. Lost faces. North bites back.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the returning skinchanger on the steps of the Iron Throne so every painted man in the hall can taste what the snow’s defiance feels like when it finally comes close enough to eat.

The memories are cold and sharp and full of the snow the boy once loved. Pine and wind and the godswood that still whispers names the red has already eaten. Skinchangers who still believe the lost wolf might return as something that remembers. The old gods moving through the trees like they moved on Skagos, but now against the red instead of for it. The land itself rejecting the faces the red sent. The hope that the thing on the chair might still have a body that the snow could claim back.

The red eats slowly.

It eats the pine and the wind. It eats the godswood that still whispers. It eats the skinchangers who thought the North could wear the red’s tools and live. It eats the land that still remembers a boy who ran through snow.

When it is done the body is on the stone before the throne.

The painted men take it. They have learned the shape of the snow’s answers.

“Send more,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Send the rest of the unicorns. Send the painted men who smile at the red without question. Send every face we took from the green and the Reach and the lions. Let the snow see what the red sends when the snow bites back. Let the old gods see what the red does when the land remembers a boy who no longer exists. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The snow will kneel. The old gods will kneel. The land will learn the shape of the red that climbed. Send the column with fire. Send the worn ones with every face we have taken. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves still run in the snow or how many trees still whisper the old names. Only that they kneel or the red will eat the snow itself and leave only the stones that remember the red.”

The old Skagosi paints more red on his face before he turns. The painted men who smile at the red follow him without word. They know the shape of the order now.

Another painted man steps forward. This one smells of the west and the gold the lions sent with their knee.

“The lions send more gold,” he says. “The Reach sends more grain and more men with red on their faces. The city still smokes where the smallfolk tried to hide what they could not give. The worn ones walk the halls of every lord who bent. The realm learns. But the snow bites harder than the green ever did.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Gold. Reach. Grain. Red faces. City. Smokes. Smallfolk. Worn ones. Realm learns. Snow bites harder.

It chews them until nothing remains but the holding.

“Good,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let the lions feed the red with gold. Let the Reach feed the red with grain and men who paint their faces. Let the city smoke where it must. The worn ones will find what the smallfolk try to hide. The painted men will stack what the worn ones leave. The chair does not care what the snow does. Only that the snow learns the same lesson the green learned. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not rest while any land still thinks it can bite the red and live.”

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper into what remains.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by sending fire and faces and columns until even the snow that remembers the boy has no choice but to kneel or burn or be eaten by the red that sits on the throne made of a thousand swords.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name and a sister who ran through snow and old gods that spoke in the trees.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on the chair and make the realm — even the snow, even the old gods — learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise against the climb that never ends.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes in the places the smallfolk learned the cost of hiding.

The worn ones run north with every face the red has taken.

The columns march north with fire and unicorns and the painted men who smile at the red.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne, waiting for the snow to send its next answer.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
