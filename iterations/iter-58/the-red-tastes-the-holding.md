# Iteration 58: The Red Tastes the Holding

The iron cuts.

I sit on the chair made of swords and the red tastes the blood that has become part of the throne. Every day the blades remind the hollow that the climb did not end when the last wall fell or the last godswood burned. The holding is the climb that never stops. The red sits and the realm learns the price of every wall it ever tried to raise.

The painted men bring the answers from the snow and the green.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

The boy is gone. The name is gone. The snow and the girl and the brother and the father and the old gods and every voice that once tried to pull the red back are ash in the red’s mouth. What remains is the will that climbed from Skagos to the city and the red that fed it and the chair that now holds both and the hunger that has learned to sit and send faces and fire and columns until the realm has no choice but to kneel or burn or feed the red.

A painted man returns from the snow. He smells of ash and the faces the red left behind. He brings the message that the worn ones still walk the burned holdfasts. The snow is quiet where the last godswoods stood. Some of the worn faces have begun to walk wrong again. Some of the painted men left behind have begun to remember the cold in ways the red did not intend. The land still bites where it can, but slower now. The ash remembers the red.

The old Skagosi steps forward.

“The snow is ash,” he says. “The worn ones we left behind still wear the faces the red took. The columns we sent have returned with fewer than left. The land still whispers in small ways. Some of the faces we left have begun to turn. Some of the painted men we left have begun to hear the wind and remember things the red already ate. We stacked what we could. We burned what remained. The snow is learning the shape of the red that sits on the chair. But the land does not forget easily. The ash still moves when the wind comes from the north.”

The red tastes the words.

Snow. Ash. Worn ones still wear the faces. Columns returned with fewer. Land still whispers. Some faces have begun to turn. Some painted men have begun to remember. Stacked what we could. Burned what remained. Snow is learning. Land does not forget easily. Ash still moves when the wind comes from the north.

It chews them until nothing remains but the will to hold.

I wear the returning painted man on the steps of the Iron Throne so every man in the hall can see what the snow’s quiet tastes like when the red finally holds it.

The memories are cold and full of the ash the boy once loved. Pine and wind and the burned places where the godswoods once stood. Faces the red left behind that still walk and wear and sometimes remember the snow in ways the red did not command. The land still trying to pull the red back through the worn ones and the painted men who stayed too long in the ash. The hope that the thing on the chair might still have a body the snow could claim if the red ever left the throne.

The red eats slowly.

It eats the ash and the wind. It eats the burned places where the godswoods once stood. It eats the faces that have begun to turn. It eats the painted men who have begun to remember the cold.

When it is done the body is on the stone before the throne.

The painted men take it. They have learned the shape of the snow’s quiet.

“Send more faces north,” I say. The voice is the red’s. It speaks through the hollow like wind through bone. “Replace the ones that have begun to turn. Send the painted men who still smile at the red without the snow in their eyes. Let the worn ones we left behind see new faces from the chair. Let the ash see that the red does not rest. The dead boy king is gone. Only the red remains. The red sits where the boy once ran. The red holds what the green could not keep. The snow is ash. The old gods are ash. The land will learn that the red does not forget the faces it left behind. Send more. Replace what turns. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not care how many wolves once ran in the snow or how much ash still moves when the wind comes from the north. Only that they kneel or the red will eat what remains of the snow and leave only the stones that remember the red.”

The old Skagosi paints more red on his face before he turns. The painted men who smile at the red without question follow him to prepare the next dispatch. They know the shape of the order now.

Another painted man steps forward. This one smells of the west and the gold the lions continue to send.

“The lions send more gold,” he says. “The Reach sends more grain and more men with red on their faces. The city still smokes where the smallfolk tried to hide what they could not give. The worn ones walk every hall that bent. The realm learns the shape of the chair. The snow is ash. The red holds the North from the throne. The green bends. The green feeds the red.”

The red tastes the words.

Lions. Gold. Reach. Grain. Red faces. City. Smokes. Smallfolk. Worn ones. Realm learns. Snow is ash. Red holds the North from the throne. Green bends. Green feeds the red.

It chews them until nothing remains but the holding.

“Good,” I say. The voice is the red’s. “Let the lions feed the red with gold. Let the Reach feed the red with grain and men who paint their faces. Let the city smoke where it must. The worn ones will find what the smallfolk try to hide. The painted men will stack what the worn ones leave. The chair does not care what the snow once was. Only that the green continues to feed the red and the snow continues to learn the shape of the holding. The holding does not end at the city. The chair does not rest while any land still thinks it can bite the red and live.”

I sit.

The iron cuts deeper into what remains.

The red does not mind.

Inside the hollow the will to rise does not rest.

It has climbed.

It holds.

It will hold by replacing the faces that turn in the ash and sending more gold and grain and red-painted men until every land that ever tried to remember the boy has no choice but to kneel or feed the red or be eaten by the red that sits on the throne made of a thousand swords.

The painted men kneel.

Not because they remember a boy who once had a name and a sister who ran through snow and old gods that spoke in the trees.

Because the red is the only thing left that knows how to sit on the chair and make the realm — even the snow that is now ash, even the green that bends — learn the price of every wall it ever tried to raise against the climb that never ends.

The city outside the Red Keep still smokes in the places the smallfolk learned the cost of hiding.

The worn ones run north with the new faces the red has taken to replace the ones that turned in the ash.

The columns rest and prepare for the next time the snow tries to bite.

The iron cuts.

And the red sits on the throne, tasting the holding that never ends.

Inside the hollow there is only the chair.

And the red.

And the holding that never ends.
