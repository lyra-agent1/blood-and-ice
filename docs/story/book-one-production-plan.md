# Book One Production Plan

## Target

Book One should be drafted in controlled packets, not as an endless generation stream.

The near-term target is a reviewable 25k-35k word opening package that proves:

- Rook's voice can carry a long first-person novel.
- The four tribes create plot without relying on outside lore.
- The White Pit is inevitable by politics, not random cruelty.
- The Wound-Guest is useful, frightening, and costly.

The long target is a full Book One of roughly 140k-180k words. A 500k-word series becomes plausible only if Book One has strong continuity systems before scaling.

## Draft Packets

### Packet 1: Wild Boy And Salt Law

**Status:** rough draft started.

Chapters:

1. Black Shore
2. Hunger Law
3. First Blood In The Trees
4. Salt Knots

Purpose:

- establish Rook's body-first voice
- introduce hunger as law
- show Salt Veyr as practical, not soft
- make Maren's decision to keep Rook alive believable
- trigger the first rumor of the red-state without explaining it

Review question:

Does Rook feel like a feral fourteen-year-old who is smart enough to learn politics, or does he sound too polished?

### Packet 2: Jurisdiction

**Status:** rough draft started.

Chapters:

5. Antlers In Fog
6. Fen Names
7. Khar Vane Demand

Purpose:

- turn Rook from survivor into contested property
- show Hollow Antler's power over meaning
- reveal Fen-Kin old-blood recognition
- make Khar Vane's demand feel like a legal move
- end with the White Pit as compromise, punishment, and test

Review question:

Does the political machine make sense without heavy exposition?

### Packet 3: The White Pit

**Status:** rough draft started.

Chapters:

8. The Descent
9. Below Hunger
10. The Boy Returns Wrong

Purpose:

- deliver the first major set piece
- make the Wound-Guest terrifying before it becomes articulate
- create multiple public interpretations of the same event
- give Khar Vane its sacred mandate

Review question:

Is the monster-state powerful enough to sell the myth but costly enough to stay dangerous?

### Packet 4: Blood Crown

**Status:** outline only.

Chapters:

11. Meat And Meaning
12. The First Restraint
13. Salt Debt
14. Antler Countermove
15. Fen Challenge
16. Bone-Mouth's Lesson
17. The Feast Dispute
18. The Second Pit
19. Blood Snow
20. The Crown That Bites

Purpose:

- prove that leadership is harder than survival
- make Khar Vane's following a trap
- show logistics beating legend
- force Rook to restrain the thing that made him useful

Review question:

Does Rook earn command through choices, or is the story letting awe do too much work?

## Chapter Standard

Each chapter should do at least three jobs:

- move the plot
- alter one relationship
- clarify one tribal rule
- deepen the Wound-Guest cost
- change Rook's public meaning
- reveal a new pressure on food, law, or ritual

If a chapter only repeats that Rook is dangerous, cut or rebuild it.

## Continuity Gates
